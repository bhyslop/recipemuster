## What needed naming

₣Ba founds a new kit between BUK and JJK: an operator-scoped, repo-shaped shared
state home ("worksite", placeholder) plus the strongly-controlled fleet-provisioning
responsibility (precise remote users, keying, normalization for reproducible
parallel testing) absorbed from BUK jurisdiction. The kit needs a register
(MCM asterism) before any of its persistent names can mint. The placeholder
"worksite" was disqualified on inspection: the `work-` stem collides audibly with
*workbench*, one of BUK's most load-bearing ashlars, and "work" is trodden
vocabulary besides. Standing anti-constraint regardless of final mint: **avoid the
`work-` stem entirely.**

## MCM rules that did the work

- *Vocabulary isolation* — source domain alien to software/cloud/CI/corporate PM.
- *Audible register* — sibling kits must be distinguishable by ear
  (JJK equestrian; RBK occult/foundry, with the federation heat actively
  colonizing Anglo-Norman manorial/legal: manor, terrier, enfranchise, escheat).
- *Mint into an asterism* — source-domain relationships must mirror system
  relationships, not merely decorate.
- *Constrains births, not the living* — an elected register governs NEW mints;
  existing words sweep only via deliberate remint, never piecemeal.
- *The grep gate* — run before adoption (results below).

## Fixed anchors (the only points minted into the soak)

- **mews** — the operator-scoped, repo-shaped home where provisioned environments
  are kept between flights. Replaces the "worksite" placeholder in paddock prose.
- **jess** — the key tether binding a provisioned user (the strap by which a
  trained hawk is held and released).

Everything else is deliberately unminted. In particular: what exactly is the hawk
(the provisioned user-environment on a node is the leading reading), what a flight
is, the kit's own name and 2–4 char prefix (namespace enumeration + terminal
exclusivity per CLAUDE.md minting workflow), and every verb.

## Open questions the soak must answer

1. Does the register survive daily voice? ("charge the mews", "jess the workload
   user onto rocket") — operator to live with it across several paddock revisions.
2. Disposition of the living foray words (fundus, curia, legatio, pensum): they
   stay JJK-owned and untouched during the soak (births-not-living). If the kit
   absorbs the responsibility they name, a deliberate remint pace decides whether
   they migrate, translate into falconry, or stand as a JJK-side dialect.
3. The hawk mapping — settle before the first verb mints, since verbs orbit it
   (sibling-initials rule applies to the verb family from birth).
4. Ashlar budget: which falconry words face the operator (broadside registration
   required) vs stay quoin/hearting.
