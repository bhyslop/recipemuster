# Image-family tabtarget retirement — terminal-exclusivity debt (heat ₣BH)

*Written 2026-06-10 while executing the image-backdoor-path-verbs pace
(₢BHAAa) in heat ₣BH (rbk-11-mvp-lode-universal-capture). Records a
deliberately-incurred terminal-exclusivity violation and the two later events
that clear it.*

TRIAGED 2026-06-10: Wave 2 confirmed landed (grep census clean — no `rbw-i[waJ]r` survives); Wave 3 recorded as an RBSHR horizon entry for the made-side retrofit heat.

## Why this memo exists

The image-backdoor-path-verbs pace introduced the generic, type-blind
path-polymorphic trio — **list / wrest / jettison** on raw GAR paths — and, by
operator decision, gave them their **final-form colophons** (the bare verb
letters) immediately rather than an interim name that would have to be
re-minted at each retirement wave:

| Colophon | Function | Register |
|----------|----------|----------|
| `rbw-il` | `rbfl_list`     | lowercase (read) |
| `rbw-iw` | `rbfl_wrest`    | lowercase (read / pull) |
| `rbw-iJ` | `rbfl_jettison` | capital (GAR-destroying) |

## The violation

CLAUDE.md "Terminal Exclusivity": *a prefix either IS a name or HAS children,
never both.* As of this pace:

- `rbw-iw` now **names** the generic wrest verb **and** still heads the live
  family `iwh` (wrest·hallmark) / `iwr` (wrest·reliquary).
- `rbw-iJ` now **names** the generic jettison verb **and** still heads the live
  family `iJh` (jettison·hallmark) / `iJr` (jettison·reliquary).
- `rbw-il` is clean — `list` had no prior `il*` family.

So `iw` and `iJ` are in violation. **This is accepted on purpose** (operator,
2026-06-10): the alternative was a throwaway suffix (e.g. `iwx`/`iJx`) minted
now and renamed twice as the families empty — churn on a permanent
primary-universe identifier across tabtargets, the zipper, docs, and tests. The
violation is transient and carries the removal conditions below.

## Removal conditions — two waves clear the debt

The image family retires in three waves tied to namespace lifecycle. Wave 1 is
this pace (retire `iwe`/`iJe`, the enshrinement variants — `rbi_es` is
disposable and fully subsumed by the generic verbs). The remaining two waves
clear the terminal-exclusivity violation:

- **Wave 2 — inscribe→conclave cutover (THIS heat, ₣BH).** When `rbi_rq`
  (reliquary) migrates into `rbi_ld`, retire the reliquary variants
  `iar` / `irr` / `iwr` / `iJr`. This removes the `iwr` / `iJr` children.
- **Wave 3 — made-side retrofit heat (SEPARATE heat, where Ark earns the `k`
  command family).** Retire the hallmark variants `iah` / `irh` / `iwh` / `iJh`.
  This removes `iwh` / `iJh` — the **last** children — and restores terminal
  exclusivity: `iw` and `iJ` become pure terminal names.

## Semantic note — not all eight remaining variants are equal

When the waves land, distinguish two kinds:

- **The four wrest/jettison variants** (`iwh` `iwr` `iJh` `iJr`) are **pure
  pass-throughs** — they already dispatch to the very same `rbfl_wrest` /
  `rbfl_jettison` the generic verbs use. Zero behavior delta. They survive only
  to retire bundled with their namespace; functionally the generic verbs cover
  them the instant they exist. Deleting these is pure tabtarget + zipper removal.
- **The four audit/rekon variants** (`iah` `iar` `irh` `irr`) are **not** mere
  redundancy. `rekon` asserts canonical-member *presence* (the
  hallmark-lifecycle fixture asserts its non-zero exit on a missing member) and
  `audit_hallmarks` emits `buf_write_fact_multi` fact files. The type-blind
  generic `list` reproduces neither. This is why `rekon` in particular is
  contracted out to the made-side retrofit heat rather than collapsed into the
  generic layer — its semantics must be re-homed, not merely deleted.
