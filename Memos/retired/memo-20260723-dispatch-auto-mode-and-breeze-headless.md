# Draft paces for heat ₣B9 — dispatch auto mode, and the breeze headless drain

Two paces drafted in a session where the JJK MCP server was absent, so neither could be
slated. Each block below is ready to paste into a `# jjezs_slate <silks>` notice; the tier
recommendation is the bridle judgment, offered for the slating session to accept or overrule.

Provenance: a conversation about whether dispatched billet sessions could run in auto mode,
and whether a bridled pace could execute headless. The first is small and ready. The second

## Pace 1 — `dispatch-auto-permission-mode`

**Recommended bridle:** sonnet. One call site, one spec section; the judgment is spent in
the docket.

### Docket body

> ## Character
> Mechanical: one launch argument, one spec paragraph, one test.
>
> Dispatched sessions launch under the vendor's default permission posture, so an operator
> answers prompts for edits inside a container built to make those edits safe. Every
> dispatched session is billet-resident by construction — its own worktree, its own branch,
> its own `BURV_*` scratch roots — so the blast radius that ordinarily argues against an
> auto-accept posture is already fenced by the billet itself.
>
> Add the auto permission posture to the composed session command in the dispatch spine's
> launch primitive (`jjrds_stirrup_command`), beside the model and effort knobs it already
> composes. Verify the vendor's accepted mode words against the installed CLI at
> implementation rather than trusting this docket's memory of them; a mismatch is a spook,
> not a guess to paper over.
>
> Record the posture in the dispatch sheaf's Session-launch section
> (`Tools/jjk/vov_veiled/JJSVD-dispatch.adoc`): the permission posture is a second vendor
> surface the spine speaks to, and it belongs beside the tier-and-effort Palisade admission
> already recorded there — same register, same reasoning, one more membrane cell. State the
> billet-as-fence rationale, since that is what makes the posture defensible rather than
> merely convenient.
>
> ## Cinched
> Unconditional in the launch primitive, not door-conditional. The billet is the fence, and
> both doors land in one; a per-door knob would open a second policy axis earning nothing.
>
> ## Done when
> A dispatched session opens without permission prompts for in-billet edits; the spine's
> launch tests assert the posture argument is composed; the dispatch sheaf records the
> posture and its billet-as-fence rationale.

## Pace 2 — `breeze-vendor-boundary-drain`

**Recommended bridle:** opus. Aspirant-sheaf authoring under MCM/AXLA law — it writes
prose downstream design paces will obey, which is exactly where a plausible-but-wrong
paragraph propagates unchecked.

### Docket body

> ## Character
> Aspirant authoring against a settled sheaf: drain a set of findings about the vendor
> session boundary into `Tools/jjk/vov_veiled/JJSAB-breeze.adoc`, mostly into slots it
> already cut.
>
> A headless-execution investigation surfaced findings about the vendor's non-interactive
> session surface that the breeze workup could not have known, because it predates that
> surface. Three land in open forks the sheaf already carries, one lands in the
> reconciliation list, and two are genuinely absent concerns. Author each in the sheaf's own
> register — candidate vocabulary, timeless prose, no vendor flag spellings (see the
> exclusion below).
>
> **Narrows the open fork on beat-completion detection.** That fork names three candidate
> signals: worktree commits, exit code, status file. Eliminate exit code by evidence: a
> non-interactive vendor session exits successfully whenever its turn completes, whatever the
> session concluded, so a clean finish and a critical finding are indistinguishable by exit
> status. Exit status is a liveness signal, never a verdict. Two candidates remain.
>
> **Adds a candidate to the open fork on provender emission convention.** That fork names
> structured block, tool call, and delimiter. The vendor surface admits a fourth: a caller
> supplies a schema constraining the session's terminal message, so the verdict arrives
> machine-typed at the boundary rather than parsed out of prose. This sits well with the
> sheaf's standing posture that the orchestrator reads structure and never interprets prose.
>
> **Adds a reconciliation bullet on designation overlap.** The existing dispatch-spine
> bullet covers launch mechanics only. It misses that the spine's tier roster and its
> two-source tier-and-effort choice already constitute what a beat row calls its model
> assignment, built and specified; and that bridling already performs a judgment rhyming
> with school's — a frontier agent ruling a docket ready and assigning a tier. Name the
> fork: is bridle a coarse-grained school over a pace, or does school subsume it at beat
> grain?
>
> **Adds a Palisade section — the sheaf has none.** The vendor session boundary returns
> verdicts that do not match reality, and the breeze design's zero-attention premise makes
> that expensive. Characterize the signature precisely: in a non-interactive session there
> is nobody to answer a permission prompt, so a prompt resolves as a denial — and the beat
> does not fail, it completes, reporting that the work could not be done as though that were
> a finding. A partially-permissioned breeze therefore degrades into confident nonsense
> rather than into the sheaf's `failed` status. Conduct at this membrane follows the
> characterize/contain/absorb/log/retire discipline. Note the scope distinction while
> writing: the sheaf top's existing Palisade quoin is the *vocabulary* Palisade (model-family
> words as an unminted admission); this is a behavioral membrane at the same edge, a sibling
> instance rather than the same cell.
>
> **Adds a bound concern — the sheaf carries none.** Breeze is zero-attention, concurrent,
> and mixed-tier, yet the beat row has no field for a turn or spend ceiling and the failure
> story is entirely semantic. A beat that never stops is the one failure mode that costs
> while nobody is watching. Record the concern and the beat-row implication; the vendor
> affords such ceilings today.
>
> **Raises a fork against a sheaf premise.** The sheaf assigns breeze to a small-model
> orchestrator and defends it with a hard gate refusing frontier-tier callers. That was
> drawn before the dispatch spine existed. The spine is deterministic engine code doing
> precisely the work breeze describes as precision-not-judgment; if breeze becomes engine
> code, the tier gate is moot by construction rather than enforced, and switchboard-not-a-
> reader stops being a discipline and becomes a type. Raise this as a fork against the
> premise — do not resolve it in this pace.
>
> ## Cinched
> No vendor flag spellings anywhere in the sheaf. The findings above are the *shape* of a
> neighbor's behavior, not its current spelling; naming argument words in an aspirant sheaf
> invites the version-commentary drift the skidmark rule exists to prevent. Characterize the
> behavior and let a membrane carry the spelling when one is built.
>
> The small-model-orchestrator question is raised, not settled. It unsettles a premise the
> sheaf rests several sections on, and settling it is its own design pace.
>
> ## Done when
> The sheaf carries a Palisade section and a bound concern; the two named open forks record
> their narrowing and their added candidate; the reconciliation list carries the designation-
> overlap bullet; the orchestrator-tier premise carries an open fork against it. No vendor
> flag spelling appears anywhere in the sheaf.
