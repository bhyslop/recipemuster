# ₣BZ Sequence Retrospective — planning lessons from the federation-create arc

Provenance, never authority. The terminal process-retrospective of heat ₣BZ
(federation-create), executed by model Fable per pace ₢BZAAh, 260701. Subject: the
SHAPE of the execution — where the planned sequence held, where it bent, and why —
not the build's substance (adjudicated at ₢BZAAN under operator sanction) and not
the ratify-or-sweep agenda (`memo-20260622-fable-review-queue.md`). Evidence: the
jjb affiliation record — 215 commits, 260604–260701, read from git's own subject
trailers after `jjx_log ₣BZ`'s full output exceeded display (same immutable
record); the as-built 32-pace roster; the heat paddock; and the heat's design
memos. Later-heat knowledge is used as outcome data, by operator leave.

## The arc

Four phases with sharp boundaries, then a tail:

1. **Gestation (0604–0612, 8 days).** Nominated as identity-credential
   convergence; the paddock curried through successive design conversations
   (citizen model, terrier, civic structure) with four silks renames and zero
   code paces. All motion was shape, memos, and diagrams.
2. **Conversion day (0612, ~5 hours).** The office-federation conversion canceled
   the keyfile citizen tier unbuilt (`memo-20260612-office-federation-conversion.md`);
   the three-leg chain was spike-proven live the same morning — ₢BZAAA wrapped
   about two hours after the conversion curry
   (`memo-20260612-federation-legs-spike-findings.md`) — and the vocabulary mint
   (₢BZAAB) wrapped within the hour after that. Spike amendments (F2, V1, V3)
   were absorbed into the paddock before any further pace was cut.
3. **Design-for-execution (0615, 1 day).** The Fable→Opus stewardship handoff:
   freeze banner at the paddock head; the pace-design membrane pace (₢BZAAC) and
   its memo (`memo-20260615-BZ-pace-design.md`); M1–M4 slated (sixteen slates in
   one day — roughly half the heat's eventual thirty-four); the 27-agent
   balanced-merits review of the M4 cuts (`memo-20260615-BZ-m4-review-findings.md`);
   M5–M7 as one-line placeholders; a known-good floor recorded (₢BZAAR, 222
   green); and M1 executed the same evening.
4. **Execution (0615–0622, 7 days).** ~28 build/proof paces. M4's eight paces
   landed 0616–0619 interleaved with proof paces and just-in-time slates; the
   M5–M7 placeholders were groomed on arrival 0620; demolition, accessor rewire,
   and the README narrative inversion closed 0621–0622; the heat stabled and the
   terminal review (₢BZAAN) ran hours later under operator sanction.

Tail: this retrospective, slated 0626, hard-gated on model Fable, run 0701.

Sequence-churn census, for scale: 34 slates (2 abandoned/transferred), 8 reslates
plus one batch, 2 rails, 8 silks renames, ~6 exercises of the operator-sanction
channel. None of this churn broke anything downstream — the churn was absorbed at
the layer designed to absorb it (pace grain), while the movement skeleton never
changed shape once (its only bends were a rail and an early pull-forward, both at
pre-visible seams).

## Where the plan held

- **The movement skeleton (the paddock's Ordering section) survived everything** —
  the conversion rewrite, the freeze, the tier handoff, sixteen just-in-time
  slates, and an external policy shock. It was the only plan artifact that never
  needed repair. The pace inventory, by contrast, was fluid by design: half the
  roster did not exist when execution began.
- **The M4 pace cuts held.** Four of the five reviewed judgment calls stood
  unchanged through execution; the one structural change the review itself made
  (the levy/affiance split) proved out — the two paces landed on different days
  against different API surfaces with the settle-gate invariant intact.
- **Freeze integrity held, even against sanctioned pressure.** The one
  operator-sanctioned paddock body edit (manor-identity, 0618) was itself
  reverted the same day to keep the freeze whole, the correction rerouted to the
  memo as a divergence pointer. The discipline was real, not ceremonial.
- **The divergence ledger ran bidirectionally.** Seventeen Opus-recorded
  divergences, zero overturned at review — and, in the other direction, the one
  place Fable's paddock was actually wrong (the "deed not a building" manor
  sense) was caught by Opus grounding-work, carried as a divergence, and
  overturned as confabulated at the sanctioned review. The same membrane that
  stopped Opus from "fixing" load-bearing Fable precision (the manufactured M4
  blocker, dissolved by reading code) also surfaced the genuine Fable error.
  Record-don't-fold-back protected both tiers from each other.

## Where it bent, and what bent it

1. **The conversion (0612)** — the largest divergence of the whole heat happened
   before execution started, while redesign was still cheap. Eight days of
   two-tier gestation were partially discarded; the civic structure, terrier,
   and vocabulary discipline survived the pivot intact.
2. **External forcing: the org keyless policy.** `disableServiceAccountKeyCreation`
   broke the keyfile bootstrap mid-heat, firing the M4/M7 review's *named revisit
   trigger* from outside. M7 work pulled forward and split into keyless-bootstrap
   + estate-demolition along what the M7 groom called "the gate sentence's own
   seam" — the plan bent exactly at a pre-scored crease, and the bend retired the
   review's one recorded residual risk early.
3. **Proof-pace discoveries.** The affiance create-shape bug ("unrun since the
   spike") fell out of the affiance-proof debugging; the workforce-pool quota
   wall (`memo-20260617-BZ-workforce-pool-constraints.md`) fell out of running
   the lifecycle round-trip — and forced the freehold/leasehold doctrine, an
   entire unplanned design layer born mid-heat that later became project-wide
   test cosmology (now spec-homed: RBSTC).
4. **Placeholder grooms (0620).** M5/M6/M7 one-liners required intent
   *recovery* — the operator did not recall M5's intent at mount, and the
   reconstruction found the one-liner had hidden a real which-axis tension. M6's
   planned position was discovered wrong (narrative before the keyless end-state
   it asserts) and railed after the demolition — cheap, precisely because
   nothing downstream had been built against the wrong order.
5. **Mid-heat mints via the sanction channel.** jilt (0616), recognosce (0617),
   gird (0620) — each pulled in because an in-heat proof needed it, each
   operator-sanctioned in conversation with recorded rationale and an explicit
   Fable-review flag (records: `memo-20260615-BZ-pace-design.md`).
6. **Splits and transfers out.** Handbook rework → ₣A6; terrier permanent
   founding-home, depot freehold, multi-foedus, credential-heal → ₣Bf; the
   headless-compearance reconsideration transferred to ₣Bf; theurge cosmology
   spec → its own pace (later the ₣Bl stream); RBS0 canon scrub → a dedicated
   pace. None blocked the heat's done-criterion; all matured later (see Outcome
   data).

## Lessons for planning future heats

**1. Plan at movement grain; slate paces just-in-time, in waves.** The durable
plan was eight one-line movements; pace detail was authored in three pulses
(conversion day, the 0615 design day, and continuously during execution as
reality demanded). Every attempt to fix detail early would have fixed the wrong
detail — M6's position, AAY's scope, the terrier pace's shape all changed on
approach. The corollary cost is lesson 2.

**2. A placeholder must carry its intent and its known tension — two sentences,
not one line.** The M5 one-liner decayed to the point of operator intent-loss and
a groom-time archaeology session; the recovered intent then revealed a contested
which-axis choice the movement had never confronted. One intent sentence plus one
"what will be contested here" sentence at slate time would have made all three
0620 grooms cheap. This is the *minimum* — full dockets authored early would have
been wrong (lesson 1), but titles alone rot in five days.

**3. When a heat pivots its architecture, the spike is not a movement — it is
hour three.** The conversion decision and its live three-leg proof landed the
same morning, before any pace was cut against the new model, and the spike's
amendments flowed into the paddock while it was still soft. Compare the cost of
the one chain that *didn't* get re-run after the spike: the affiance create path
sat unexercised for five days and shipped a wrong REST shape that the proof pace
had to debug live.

**4. Freeze discipline is worth keeping — but plan the thaw and structure the
overflow channel at freeze time.** What worked: corrections routed to a memo,
divergences recorded not folded, integrity actively defended (the 0618 revert),
and a terminal review with a clean substrate. What cost: the nominal thaw
("Fable re-authors") never happened — operator sanction was always the realistic
path and should be named as primary, not fallback; and the correction memo grew
into a 435-line chronological second paddock that needed a mid-flight
consolidation pass and a whole successor memo (the review queue) to route its
durables home. Prescription: when the freeze banner lands, the companion memo
gets a fixed section skeleton on day one (corrections / divergences / new design
/ mint ledger), and the thaw condition is written as the operator-sanction event
it will actually be. Expect and budget the three-surface read cost (frozen
paddock + memo + queue) for every late-heat mount; the 260625 three-heat audit
(`memo-20260625-three-heat-audit-process.md`) independently confirms that
provenance hygiene — stale pointers, prose written against demolished states —
is *the* systematic defect class of heavily-restructured plans, so the overflow
channel is where audit attention should concentrate.

**5. Divergence budgeting = named revisit triggers on every non-high-confidence
call.** The heat's single medium-confidence sequencing call carried a named
trigger; when external reality fired it, the plan bent at the pre-scored seam
with no thrash — the alternative reading had already been designed. Seventeen
recorded divergences never paid out as overturns, but the ledger was cheap and
bought the terminal review its entire agenda; the trigger, by contrast, paid out
in full. The asymmetry is the lesson: recording *decisions* is insurance,
recording *triggers* is contingency design. The latter is the load-bearing half.

**6. Split-vs-absorb had a consistent implicit rule — make it explicit.** Absorb
when the new work sits on the critical path of an in-heat proof (jilt ← the
affiance round-trip; gird ← the autonomous gauntlet; recognosce ← levy
verification; the freehold substrate ← in-heat fixtures). Split when it is
evolution or consolidation that does not block the done-criterion (terrier
founding-home, depot freehold, handbook, cosmology spec, canon scrub). Every
absorb was operator-sanctioned with a recorded scope note; every split named its
destination heat. Zero regrets visible in the record on either side.

**7. Proof paces are where reality enters — plan them per movement, and plan
their demolition.** Proofs found what build paces could not (the create-shape
bug, the quota wall, the probe-family regression) and *generated design* (the
freehold doctrine). The heat also demonstrated the full lifecycle: the faked-don
proofs were deliberately retired as spent scaffolding (₢BZAAd) once real dons
existed — scaffolding-with-a-demolition-condition applied to test rigs
themselves.

**8. Run deference and challenge as separate channels, and spend review where
behavior changes.** Stewardship ran deferential ("suspect the divergence is
Opus's first") and that prior prevented a wrong turn on day one; structural
review ran explicitly balanced-merits (27 agents, ~3.1M tokens, on M4 only — the
behavior-changing movement) and produced the one structural change plus the one
trigger that mattered. Neither posture would have served the other's job: a
deferential reviewer misses the levy split; a challenging steward manufactures
blockers. The zero-overturn count is not evidence of steward servility — the
challenge channel existed, was licensed, and drew blood where it was aimed.

**9. Tier vocabulary-mint review by traffic (outcome data).** The two words later
reminted are exactly the two high-traffic error-surface ashlars the queue flagged
as risky (compear → avow; assize → sitting — both live in `rba_auth.sh` today),
while the low-traffic ceremony words (jilt, gird, affiance, brevet, unseat,
attaint, muniment, rehearse) all ratified as minted. Prescription: a high-traffic
ashlar deserves the full probe *before* code lands (the remint sweeps were paid
twice); low-traffic founding-ceremony words can safely ride the
provisional-with-sanction pipeline this heat used.

## The standing provenance question, answered

`memo-20260615-BZ-pace-design.md` (Manor-identity divergence, 260618) left the
provenance call — Fable confabulation vs spec/impl drift — "for Fable," noting it
bears on whether paddock authoring needs a spec-conformance gate. Ruling:
confabulation-dominant blend. The "deed not a building" coinage is
generative-prose-shaped, appeared nowhere in spec or code, and arrived during a
large-scale paddock recast — the exact conditions under which a fluent author
invents a clean-sounding identity for a concept it did not re-ground. The drift
hypothesis explains the *opportunity* (no conformance check existed), not the
*artifact*. The process consequence is cheap and worth adopting: **a paddock
curry that recasts the identity of a spec-homed concept must carry the quoin
citation at the recast site, or flag itself as proposed-not-grounded.**
Grep-before-recast, the authoring-side twin of the grep gate on mints. Flagged
below for a home.
