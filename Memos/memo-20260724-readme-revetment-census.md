# README Revetment Census

2026-07-24. Produced by pace ₢B2·B2AAB (readme-revetment-census).
Source map for the README-binding decision: every glossary entry and narrative-spine
section of `README.md` mapped to its interior home, span class, and any content

as read on this date; line numbers will drift — the file references are the durable part.

## Method and definitions

The face is `README.md` — RB's revetment (MCM `mcm_revetment`, whose type specimen
is a project's public README) and simultaneously RB's broadside (the ashlar registry).
The interior is the veiled codex: `RBS0-SpecTop.adoc` and its ~95 included sheaves,
plus cross-kit homes in BUS0 and the committed moorings config.

Span classes (per the pace's cinch — assigned per span, never per section):

- **extracted** — a tagged gloss transcluded from its home by machinery.
- **attested** — authored prose bound to an identifiable home; alignment is by
  discipline (re-blessed when the home moves), not by machinery.
- **face-primary** — the face IS the home; no interior document says this content.

"Interior" means cipher-carrying AND veiled-homed; names on the distributed surface
(config files, RELEASE.md, BUK's shipped docs) are homes but not veiled ones, and
their appearance on the face is not a spall.

Headline structural facts:

- README carries **129 anchors** (`<a id=`), of which ~105 are glossary-entry
  anchors (operations, roles, artifacts, regimes, nameplates) and the rest
  section/appendix anchors.
- **No transclusion machinery exists.** Nothing generates or consumes README —
  no `tag::` regions, no renderer, no zipper involvement. The *extracted* class
  is therefore **empty today**: every span on the face is attested or face-primary.
- Nearly every glossary term resolves to a registered quoin in RBS0's mapping
  section (`rbtgo_*` operations, `rbtf_*` federation civics, `rbtga_*` artifacts,
  `rbsc_*` crucible verbs, `rbtr_*` roles, `rbtgi_*` infrastructure) — the
  interior vocabulary layer is in materially better health than the pace's
  framing feared. The binding gap is real but it is a *freshness* gap on
  operational facts, not a *home-existence* gap on vocabulary.

## Contradiction register

The load-bearing measurement. Found by comparing face claims against their homes
and against live config/code during mapping — the day-one-wrongness class that no
staleness check catches. Face defects first, then interior defects found en route.

**F1 — Charge order reversed (major).**
README's Charge entry: "brings up the Crucible in dependency order: Pentacle
creates the namespace, Sentry configures policy, then the Bottle starts."
RBSCC specifies (and compose implements): networks → **Sentry** (health) →
**Pentacle** (health) → Bottle, "guaranteeing that security enforcement is active
before the bottle starts." The face's own Pentacle paragraph states the correct
order ("the Sentry's rules are already live when the Pentacle prepares the
namespace") — so the face contradicts both the spec and itself. Major because the
first-packet-enforcement ordering is the heart of the security narrative.

**F2 — srjcl "academic-domain allowlist" (major, stated twice).**
Reference Nameplates entry and the Reference Project tree row both say
academic-domain allowlist; `rbmm_moorings/srjcl/rbrn.env` sets
`RBRN_UPLINK_ALLOWED_DOMAINS=anthropic.com`. Flatly wrong on a checkable fact.

**F3 — ccyolo "Anthropic-only" (minor).**
The allowlist also carries `www.internic.net` (and ICANN-range CIDRs) beside the
Anthropic domains. "Anthropic-only" overstates; the DNS-bootstrap entry is policy,
not an accident.

**F4 — Abjure enumeration (moderate).**
Face: "the `-image`, `-about`, and `-vouch` tags deleted as a coherent unit."
RBSAA: "Abjuring an ark deletes **every package** discovered beneath the hallmark
subtree" — five basenames including `pouch` and `diags`. The face understates the
deletion scope.

**F5 — Terrier writer list (minor).**
Face: "written by Brevet and Unseat." Gird also writes a muniment (RBSPG's
muniment-first composition), Attaint sweeps, Escheat sweeps dead records.

**F6 — Lede dependency list (minor, curated).**
Face lede: "needs only `bash 3.2`, `curl`, `openssl`, `jq`, and a handful of
standard tools." RBS0 Consumer Dependencies additionally *requires* `git`,
`docker`, `ssh`/`scp`/`ssh-keygen`, `tee`. The hedge arguably covers the standard
tools, and Docker is stated prominently elsewhere on the face — but the lede's
list and the spec's table are two hand-maintained enumerations of the same fact,
and they have already diverged once (RBSCO's third variant, see I1).

**F7 — Vouch verifier locus (ambiguity, resolved consistent).**
The lede says "no Python runtime"; the Provenance appendix says verification uses
"Python standard library and `openssl` only." Both are true — the DSSE
verification runs *inside Cloud Build* (`rbfv_verify.sh`: "All modes use Cloud
Build for vouch") — but the face never says the verifier is cloud-side, so the
two spans read as contradicting. One clause fixes it.

**I1 — RBSCO is dead as the face's source (major, triage-driving).**
RBSCO self-describes as "source-of-truth for writers porting into README.md" and
performs none of that role: its dependency list (`bash, git, curl, openssh, jq`;
no `openssl`) contradicts both the face and RBS0 (it predates the openssl+curl
REST path and the federation era); its framing ("purely an open-source
undertaking") predates the current posture; its accomplished-infrastructure
section is dated history that belongs to RBSPH. Structurally it is orphaned —
not `include::`d by RBS0 — and it carries **no sheaf voicing at all**, despite
being AXLA's registered `axd_cosmology` exemplar (AXLA genre census names
"RBSCO, RBSTC"). Whatever the triage, AXLA's exemplar row is touched — as the
pace's Bearings anticipated.

**I2 — RBSHR podman staleness + dangling refs (minor, interior).**
The roadmap sheaf — the home the face's Roadmap appendix attests to — opens its
podman entry "Recipe Bottle currently deploys to Podman on workstations via the
bottle lifecycle (RBSBS, RBSBR, RBSBL)": the deployment claim is pre-Docker-cutover
stale, and RBSBS/RBSBR resolve to no files (only RBSBC/RBSBK/RBSBL exist).

### What the count means

The distribution is the finding. **Zero contradictions were found in the
vocabulary layer** — the ~60 term definitions agree in meaning with their quoin
homes everywhere checked. Every contradiction lives in the **operational-fact
layer**: an ordering claim, a config characterization, an enumeration, a writer
list, a dependency roster. These are exactly the spans that drift when config or
mechanism evolves and nobody re-blesses the prose — F2 is a config edit the face
never followed; F1 is likely an early narrative that the spec outgrew.

Priced against the cuts: the Full cut's expensive machinery — gloss relocation
into ~44 operation sheaves, block-grain AXLA markers, a renderer — targets the
gloss layer, where nothing was found wrong. Mechanical extraction would have
prevented **none** of F1–F7: no found defect is a divergence between a gloss and
its spec text; each is authored prose making a claim about mechanism or config in
its own words. What *would* have caught F1–F5 is exactly what caught them here:
a reader walking span against home — attestation discipline, run once. What would
keep them caught is a re-bless trigger when homes move, which is a discipline (or
at most a lightweight ledger), not a pipeline.

## Scope recommendation

**Take the Minimal cut, plus a face-repair pace. The census does not support the
Full cut.**

Minimal, fitting this heat:

1. **Reveille spall gate** for the mechanically-checkable face invariants:
   every `[X](#Y)` resolves to a declared `<a id="Y">` (the references-resolve
   invariant — fully mechanical, 129 anchors); no cipher-carrying veiled name or
   veiled basename appears on the face (the ashlar-or-prose invariant's
   mechanical shadow — the same shape the pyx release-hygiene fixture already
   proves for delivered files). The other two invariants (broadside-complete,
   register-silence) are only partially mechanizable; the gate takes the
   mechanical halves and discipline keeps the rest.
2. **RBSCO triage** (I1): retire it to provenance or gut it to an honest
   informative cosmology voiced `//axvd_sheaf axd_informative axd_cosmology` —
   either way its false source-of-truth charter goes, RBSPH keeps the history,
   and AXLA's `axd_cosmology` exemplar row is corrected in the same stroke.
   Operator's pick; the census only establishes that the current state — an
   unvoiced, orphaned sheaf claiming a role it does not perform — is the worst
   of the options.
3. **Face-repair pace** for F1–F7 (one small pace: seven localized prose edits,
   two of them one-liners), and an interior touch-up for I2 in RBSHR. F1 and F2
   are worth doing promptly — both are wrong about security-relevant facts on
   the public face.
4. **Glossary stays hand-written in README** as the broadside, aligned by
   discipline. The vocabulary layer's clean census is the evidence this is
   working.

The Full cut — relocation, block-grain markers, renderer, attestation machinery,
minting-workflow change across three kits — is not argued for by anything found.
It solves gloss drift, and gloss drift is the one failure mode this census
observed **zero** instances of. It also does not fit this heat's small-cleanups
charter; if future censuses (this document is repeatable — the checks are cited)
begin finding gloss-meaning drift, that evidence would nominate a dedicated
revetment-machinery heat. Not before.

A third cut was considered and is *not* recommended as a pace now, but is named
for honesty: a **per-span attestation ledger** (span → home → home-hash,
re-bless on mismatch) would have flagged F2 the day `srjcl/rbrn.env` changed,
and is the only Full-cut element with observed value. Its price is a new
maintained artifact and a new gate; against a base rate of ~2 config-drift
defects per ~130 spans, the census judges discipline (nameplate prose quotes its
allowlist rather than characterizing it) the better buy. Revisit if the face
grows more config-characterizing spans.
