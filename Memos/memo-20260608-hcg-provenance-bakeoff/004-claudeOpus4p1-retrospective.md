# Retrospective: Creating Digital Mind Primary Documents

## Discovery

We discovered a collaborative pattern where documents are:
- **Authored by** the Digital Mind (LLM)
- **Interpreted by** the Digital Mind in future sessions
- **Approved by** the Editor (human) based on outcomes, not deep reading
- **Refined through** backpressure when procedures reveal gaps

This inverts traditional documentation where humans write for humans to read.

## The Pattern

### 1. Role Inversion Recognition

Start by identifying when the Digital Mind will be the primary interpreter:
- Repetitive creative tasks (procedure writing, report generation)
- Complex pattern matching across domains
- Tasks where human oversight focuses on outcomes, not process

Ask: "Who needs to deeply understand this document to do their job?"

### 2. Interview Process

The Editor interviews the Digital Mind about the domain:
- Editor provides context and constraints
- Digital Mind asks clarifying questions
- Editor answers based on outcomes needed, not implementation details
- Multiple Q&A rounds before drafting

Critical: The Editor explicitly states when they haven't read predecessor documents, maintaining the boundary.

### 3. Efficiency Over Explanation

Since Digital Minds have token limits:
- Trust existing intuitions (don't explain what the model already knows)
- Add guidance only where domain-specific needs diverge from intuitions
- Use terse markers for complex concepts (like guillemets for boundary-marking)

### 4. Vocabulary Development

Create specialized notation that:
- Separates meta-layer from execution-layer
- Marks Digital Mind concerns vs Editor concerns
- Enables quick pattern recognition

Example: The guillemet notation emerged naturally to mark "things that need substitution."

### 5. Backpressure Design

Build in evolution mechanisms:
- Editor identifies issues in outputs, not in the guide
- Editor requests changes without needing to understand implementation
- Digital Mind proposes specific guide updates
- Editor approves based on improved outcomes

## Key Insights

**"Wake up efficiently"**: These documents help a fresh Digital Mind quickly reach the right mental state for the task.

**Minimalism serves multiple audiences**: Terse guides work because:
- Digital Minds parse efficiently
- Editors approve quickly
- Output procedures remain readable

**Trust intuitions, guide edges**: Most patterns already exist in the Digital Mind's training. Only specify where your domain differs.

**Outcome focus**: The Editor never needs to become an expert in the guide itself, only in recognizing good outputs.

## Anti-patterns We Avoided

- **Over-explaining to the Digital Mind**: "Here's what a procedure is..."
- **Editor-focused documentation**: Long explanations for human understanding
- **Rigid prescriptivism**: "Always do X" instead of heuristics
- **Mixed audiences**: Trying to serve both Editor and Digital Mind equally

## Questions for Future Guides

When creating a new Digital Mind primary document:

1. **Role clarity**: Is the Digital Mind the primary interpreter? Will the Editor mainly review outputs?

2. **Intuition assessment**: What does the Digital Mind already understand well? Where does domain-specific guidance add value?

3. **Notation needs**: What boundaries or concepts need special markers?

4. **Evolution path**: How will the Editor identify needed improvements without deep document knowledge?

5. **Token efficiency**: Can we wake up the Digital Mind in under 2000 tokens?

## The Collaboration Shape

```
Editor (domain knowledge) 
    ↓ [interview]
Digital Mind (pattern recognition)
    ↓ [drafts guide]
Meta-Specification
    ↓ [interprets]
Digital Mind (future session)
    ↓ [creates output]
Procedure/Output
    ↓ [reviews]
Editor (approves/requests changes)
    ↓ [backpressure]
Meta-Specification (evolves)
```

## Success Indicators

You've achieved the pattern when:
- The Editor trusts outputs without reading the guide
- New Digital Mind sessions quickly produce appropriate outputs
- Refinements focus on edge cases, not fundamental restructuring
- The guide remains stable across many usage cycles

## Final Observation

This pattern works because it respects the different intelligences involved. The Digital Mind excels at pattern matching and consistency; the Editor excels at recognizing appropriate outcomes. Neither needs to do the other's job.

The meta-specification becomes a bridge between these intelligences, written in the Digital Mind's "native language" while serving the Editor's goals.
