# Fast-tier credlessness is convention, not construction — near-miss record

Date: 2026-06-10
Status: standing-hazard record from the podvm refresh pace; the immediate instance was caught
pre-run and fixed with a buo test-seam. Triage material for the ₣BH terminal memo-walk pace.
TRIAGED 2026-06-10: slated as the fast-tier-credless-by-construction pace (₢BHAAl, pending — the poison-marker enforcement).

## The near-miss

The fast-tier podvm resolve case invokes the real `rbw-lI` tabtarget against the real working
regime — theurge isolates only BURV temp/output dirs, never credentials. On a credentialed
workstation (the operator's always is), the case would have sailed past its asserted diagnostic
into a live multi-GB Cloud Build submission, inside the `fast` suite, on every run.
Caught by inspection before any run; fixed by short-circuiting the verb under a
`buorb_immure_resolve_only` tweak.

## The standing hazard

Nothing structural makes the fast tier credless. Every existing fast fixture is safe only
because none happened to invoke a GCP-capable colophon — the first one that did nearly fired a
live build. Future fast cases can repeat this silently: the failure mode is a *passing* fast
suite that spends money and mutates the depot.

## Triage question

Whether to enforce fast-tier credlessness by construction. Candidate shapes (not designed, just
recorded): the fast suite runner exports a poison marker that the credential-load path refuses
(loud "fast tier reached a credential load" die); or theurge's fast tier asserts no network
egress; or a per-case declaration ("reaches GCP: yes/no") validated against the tier. Each has
costs; the buo-seam-per-case convention may also be judged sufficient — but then the convention
needs stating somewhere an author of a new fast case will read it.
