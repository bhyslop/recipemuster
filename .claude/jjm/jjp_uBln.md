## Paddock: rbk-99-aspirant-microvm

Concept home: `Tools/rbk/vov_veiled/RBSCV-crucible_variant.adoc` (the aspirant sheaf), mounted under RBS0 `== Aspirants`.
The sheaf is the authority for this heat — shape and reason-to-believe live there; this paddock points, it does not restate.

This heat is **stabled by design**: the aspirant bar (reason-to-believe-possible) is met, and both the concrete design and all word-minting are deferred.
It is a parking place for the concept until a trigger makes it actionable.

Cinched in the founding design conversation (see the sheaf for the full frame):

- A crucible is a *strong definition of a security arrangement*; the container-namespacing topology is a means, not the end.
- The arrangement is two contracts (file-exposure, network-containment) realized upon a swappable *isolation substrate* (shared-kernel container ↔ per-workload microVM).
- The sentry (network-containment enforcer) survives every substrate swap — it lives on the network axis, orthogonal to isolation.
- The pentacle dissolves under the microVM binding (born-constrained becomes an internet-less-network attribute); it is a means, never a config item.
- Substrate strength is per-role, driven by whose code the role runs (foreign in the Subject, operator-owned in the Enforcer) — mixed-substrate by design.
- A nameplate is not a multiplatform promise; the microVM crucible is a *variant* authored on need. No regime knob is decided.

Reason-to-believe (demonstrated, not designed): Apple `container` on macOS 26 realizes the sole-egress-through-a-dual-homed-enforcer topology on a per-workload-microVM substrate (apple/container Discussion #719); Firecracker/libkrun is the Linux binding.
