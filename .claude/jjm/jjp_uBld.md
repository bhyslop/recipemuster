## Charter

Bring the concept-model corpus's annotation surface to health under the current AXLA/MCM law:
the voicing grammar moved to prefix-discriminated motifs and the mutability-phase trichotomy landed as staged doctrine,
but the corpus still carries the superseded authorship form and undeclared phases,
and the axig level taxonomy still classifies by unlinked lead-line prose.
Three paces: a mint verdict on the level taxonomy,
corpus-wide eviction of the legacy voicing form,
and the mutability-phase retrofit rollout.

## Cinched

- The level-taxonomy question is a mint decision, not a curation pass —
  the motivating emphasis survey found zero emphasis-vs-quoin drift, so nothing is mechanically convertible;
  if the verdict is to mint, the AXLA source edits are additive elevation in a follow-on pace, never folded into the verdict pace.
- The legacy-form eviction is pure form migration: motif and dimension terms unchanged,
  only the keyword dropped and the motif promoted to lead; no re-voicing rides along.
- The type-scheme linter AXLA anticipates is a forward reference to the matricula validator, which does not exist yet:
  this heat retrofits declarations and banks enforcement obligations at their spec homes; it builds no linter.
- When a legacy form is evicted, the guidance restatements of it (claude-*.md includes, MCM descriptions)
  reduce to historical notes in the same act —
  an eviction that leaves live guidance describing the dead form has not finished.
- The mutability-phase retrofit is incremental — coverage grows pace by pace, never a big-bang sweep.

## Done when

The level-taxonomy verdict is recorded, with any mint allocated per the minting workflow;
no legacy voicing annotation survives outside historical notes, guidance restatements swept;
existing axr_record voicings declare exactly one mutability phase corpus-wide,
with enforcement banked at the matricula validator home rather than built here.
