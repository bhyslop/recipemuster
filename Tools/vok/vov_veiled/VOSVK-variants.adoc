== Charter

A *variant kind* is one way a quoin's display surface inflects — number, possession, tense — as distinct from the quoin's identity, which never varies.
This sheaf homes the roster of kinds, the declaration law, and the letter-election slots, so that no consumer re-derives them and no two consumers drift.

== Founding Census (decision record, 260720)

No enumeration existed anywhere before this sheaf.
The AsciiDoc-era declared sets disagree by spec — MCM and RBS0 declare four kinds (`_s`, `_p`, `_ed`, `_ing`); BUS0, AXLA, JJS0, and VOS0 declare two (`_s`, `_p`) — and live usage across the veiled canons at the census ran plural 211, possessive 49, past 2, progressive 1, plus one `_es` and one `_ies` (suffix-shaped counts, approximate).
The `_es`/`_ies` irregularity is not a kind: it is a spelling of plural, forced into the identifier because AsciiDoc encoded the variant there.
Under Diptych the kind lives in the pilcrow key and the spelling in the declaration, so that whole irregularity class dies.

== The Roster

Closed, centrally amended — a new kind is a deliberate amendment to this sheaf, never a discovery (the precept-terminal-letter posture).

* *base* — the quoin's undeclined display text; its spelling is the display text itself, so it homes with the definition-site fork (VOSYD) rather than a declarator.
* *plural* — noun kind.
* *possessive* — noun kind.

Unadmitted until a real specimen demands: plural-possessive, third-person-singular, comparative and superlative, any further inflection.
The founding census shows the verb kinds nearly theoretical — three uses repo-wide — retained because they are already declared law in MCM and RBS0, not because usage earns them; a settling-time review may demote them.

== Laws

* *Declared, never derived.*
  Every kind's spelling is authored per quoin at its definition site (Codex to Codices, Task Lens to Task Lenses).
  No consumer ever inflects mechanically: irregular morphology is the norm on this vocabulary, not the exception, and the codec stays dumb.
* *Absent means unavailable.*
  A kind not declared for a quoin cannot be referenced; a reference operator naming an undeclared kind rejects loud — a validator rule for free.
* *One string per quoin.*
  The reference payload is always the bare immutable quoin; the kind rides only the key.
* *Grimoire alignment.*
  Grimoire rows key their form columns on this roster.
  Where a single-word quoin's declared spelling and its word's grimoire row overlap, the matricula may run an advisory exact-match agreement lint (VOr_m7w-clean); the two homes answer different questions — display text of a reference here, surface forms of a word there — so the overlap is checked, never collapsed.
  Grimoire assists authorship (its mint-gate row is the source a session copies declarations from) and is never a read-time dependency: the studbook never distributes, and a canon must render from a bare clone.
