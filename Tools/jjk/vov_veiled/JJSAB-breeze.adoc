== What breeze is

A pace enters a schooled state when a frontier-tier planning session — *school* — reads its
docket, investigates the codebase, and consults the *gait library* (the option set of reusable
work recipes) to carve a *beat map*: a concrete table of dispatch units, each carrying a fully
resolved prompt, a model assignment, a file scope, and a concurrency layer.
The *breeze* process then executes that map with zero human attention, parceling beats out to
variously configured and targeted LLM sessions — concurrently within a layer, sequentially
across layers — collecting results and informational output as it goes.
A human returns only at *corral*, reviewing the composed outcome per pace.

The system is designed around human attention as the bottleneck: the point is not raw
parallelism but a frictionless serial path for the operator, with the machine absorbing
everything mechanical between the judgment moments.

== The four phases and the attention model

[cols="1,2,4,1"]
|===
| Phase | Tier | What it does | Attention

| Longe
| read-only, parallel
| Heat-level readiness assessment: classifies every remaining pace as breezable /
  needs-refinement / blocked, guiding where groom effort goes before school commits.
  Reads each pace's *chain* — inter-pace dependency metadata set at slate/reslate time, never
  discovered downstream — as a readiness input.
| low

| School
| frontier, conversational
| Per-pace planning: assesses docket quality against codebase reality, evaluates confidence
  gates, then writes the beat map — concrete beat entries with resolved warrants, chukker
  assignments, and model selections.
  Its primary discipline is *refusing to plan on weak foundations*: the most valuable output may
  be "I cannot warrant this docket — here's what's wrong."
| high

| Breeze
| orchestrator (small-model)
| Executes the beat map chukker by chukker: merges prior work, assembles informational context,
  dispatches warrants to per-beat sessions, collects results and provender, updates status.
  Precision, not judgment.
| zero

| Corral
| human + LLM, per-pace
| Reviews the composed pace outcome (not individual beats) against the docket.
  Three outcomes: accept; refine interactively then accept; reject — rejection flows upstream to
  groom/reslate and eventual re-school. There is no rebreeze; the fix is always upstream.
| medium
|===

The refinement cycle: longe identifies readiness → human grooms unready paces → school warrants
ready paces → breeze executes → corral reviews → longe reassesses.

== Candidate vocabulary

The word *beat* is already Lapidary-reserved for this vocabulary (the AXLA interaction unit was
deliberately named away from it); the rest is candidate only.

[cols="1,4"]
|===
| Word | Meaning (candidate)

| Gait
| Reusable recipe of beats organized by chukker; can compose other gaits (practical depth ~2).
  A *school-time* resource: school reads gaits for decomposition guidance and confidence gates,
  then produces concrete beat entries.
  Breeze never looks up a gait.

| Quirt
| Gait identity — an immutable snapshot.
  Evolution mints a new quirt with the same silks; "latest version" = highest quirt with matching
  silks; no lineage fields.

| Beat
| Abstract step within a gait (template with a piaffe) *and* concrete row in the beat map
  (dispatch unit with a resolved warrant).
  The home disambiguates: gait library vs execution table.

| Piaffe
| The prose field of an abstract gait beat template: purpose, approach, and nosebag declarations
  (produces/expects).
  School reads piaffes to write concrete warrants.

| Warrant
| The resolved prompt school writes into one beat row: concrete instructions for one model
  dispatch, no symbols left to resolve, citing its source quirt for audit.
  The escalation runs docket → piaffe → warrant: intent → template → concrete.

| Chukker
| Numbered concurrency layer within a volte: all beats in chukker N execute concurrently,
  chukkers execute in sequence.
  One integer per beat — deliberately simpler than an arbitrary dependency DAG.

| Volte
| One attempt at executing one or more paces; one active volte per heat.
  School creates it, breeze executes it, corral reviews it.

| Martingale
| Volte identity (candidate glyph `₼`).

| Kimberwick
| Beat-instance identity, scoped to its martingale (candidate glyph `₭`).
  If school exhausts the slot space, breeze and corral what exists — not an error.

| Provender
| A beat's informational output: one optional markdown document, recorded indelibly on the beat
  row when status → complete, scoped to the martingale (dies with a rejected volte).

| Nosebag
| A `##`-labeled subsection within a provender document — the addressable unit of informational
  flow.
  Consuming beats declare expected nosebag names in their piaffe; the orchestrator matches `##`
  headers across prior completed beats and delivers matching sections at dispatch time.
  Name-matched, never ID-matched; the orchestrator reads structure, never interprets prose.

| Blaze
| Symbolic document/resource reference (a ₿-sigiled role word) resolved by school to concrete
  paths — breeze never sees symbols.
  Canon home: the tackle table's blaze registries (`JJSVT-tackle.adoc`) — one namespace per
  table (the workup's per-heat and per-tackle scopes yield);
  gaits reference role words, and school resolves against the table in play.
|===

Three naming regimes coexist: minted prefixes (code and specs), silks (gallops entities —
heats, paces, gaits), and blazes (₿-sigiled role words in the tackle table's registries,
resolved at school time).
Tackles sit outside all three: rows key on plain spoken names and nothing durable references
a tackle (the names-not-identities ruling, canon at `JJSVT-tackle.adoc`), so the identity
ladder below carries no tackle rung.

== The beat map

Beats are a flat collection, each carrying an immutable pace reference — paces do not contain
beats; beats point to their pace.
Each row carries: identity (kimberwick), pace reference, warrant, model assignment, file scope
(empty = read-only reviewer beat), chukker number, source quirt, status, and provender.

Status has three states only — pending / complete / failed — with no "dispatched" state:
crash recovery is idempotent re-dispatch of pending beats.
Provender is the only field that changes after beat creation: set once, indelibly, on
completion.

The V4 workup placed the beat table in gallops rather than in git branches ("lane isolation" —
a natural state store with granular status), and held an identity ladder where symbol length
alone disambiguates (2 = firemark, 3 = quirt, 4 = martingale, 5 = coronet, 6 = kimberwick).
Both choices re-open under the substrate reconciliation below, and the identity ladder must
also reconcile with the pace-identity re-gestalt landing in the studbook–farrier heat.

== Execution mechanics (as designed pre-substrate)

Recorded as the V4-era design; the substrate reconciliation below governs what survives.

*Chukker sequencing.*
Dispatch all beats in chukker N concurrently; wait for completion or failure; advance.
At each boundary: mechanical merge of prior-chukker work, provender collection, nosebag
assembly for the beats about to dispatch.

*Branch-per-beat, just-in-time.*
Branches are execution artifacts, not planning artifacts: created when a beat's chukker becomes
active, from a staging point produced by merging completed prior-chukker branches.
Merge conflict at a boundary is a fail-fast signal for human attention.

*Two kinds of merge.*
Mechanical merge is orchestrator infrastructure (git-level, automatic at every boundary).
Semantic merge is an explicit beat a gait may name — an LLM reading parallel outputs for
consistency, receiving assembled context: warrants (intent), beat diffs (outputs), the docket
(goal), and prior nosebags.
Tackle integrity declarations (builds, tests, linters) inform whether mechanical merge with
checks suffices or an alignment beat is needed.

*Failure and recovery.*
Failed = critical issue needing human attention; open questions remain on whether a failed beat
re-arms in place or only through a fresh volte.
Completed provender survives crashes (already indelible); orphan worktrees are swept.

== Concurrent dispatch

The V4-era model dispatches one authenticated agent session per beat, each in its own worktree,
launches staggered a second or two apart to soften rate-limit pressure.
Different model tiers draw on independent rate pools, so mixed-tier beat maps get more effective
throughput than a single-tier map.
Model-tier enforcement is a hard gate, not an honor system: every orchestrator invocation
carries a self-reported model identity, and breeze-phase commands refuse frontier-tier callers —
conducting is small-model work; judgment is reserved for school and corral.

*Bare-prompt reproducibility* is the scientific core: same prompt + same model + same worktree
state = comparable output.
A multi-turn conversation is path-dependent; a bare warrant is a function call.
When a beat produces bad output, change one variable — the prompt or the model — and rerun; the
closed loop (prompt → output → evaluate → refine) is what makes the gait library a learning
system.
Tool use during beat execution is expected but opaque to the orchestrator: it dispatches one
warrant and collects commits.

The orchestrator handles state and sequencing; the sessions handle work and judgment.
It emits directives, never interprets warrants or provender prose — a switchboard, not a
reader.

== The gait library

Gaits live as data, identified by quirt.
A gait is a playbook the coach consults before writing the game plan — the plan itself (the
beat map) says exactly what each player does, never "run play #47."

A well-formed gait beat template carries a piaffe (purpose, approach, nosebag produces/expects,
resources), and may carry auto-tool steps (builds, tests, linters) and quality requirements
(retry-up-to-N or fail-fast).
*Confidence gates are the most valuable part* — they encode where this kind of work goes wrong,
and school evaluates them before committing to a beat map.

The library grows from practice, not design: start with a few single-beat gaits, use them,
crystallize recurring compositions as named gaits, and let each new quirt version encode
lessons from prior bare-prompt outcomes.
Gait selection belongs in school, not at slate time.

== Reconciliation with the revision-control substrate

The standing design obligation.
The V4 workup was drawn before the studbook, farrier, blotter, and dispatch layer existed;
each collision below is a design decision for a future pace, not a defect in either side:

* *Beat worktrees vs billets* — branch-per-beat with `jj/martingale/kimberwick` naming predates
  the billet lifecycle (dirname = branch name, nuke-on-start sweep, liveness join).
  A beat's execution container is plausibly just a billet; the sweep and retention story should
  be one story, not two.
* *Beat map home vs the blotter* — "warrants in gallops for lane isolation" predates the
  studbook as the gallops' physical vessel and the journal ceremony as the sole write bracket.
  Where volte execution state lives — and how concurrent beat completions record — must be
  re-derived over the blotter's single-writer-under-lock model.
* *One-active-volte vs the guidon* — the volte mutex and the lock discipline should rhyme.
* *Dispatch vs the dispatch spine* — per-beat session launch must ride jjy_saddle / jjy_lunge
  and the entrance spine (identify, pedigree lookup, billet-ensure, context provisioning)
  rather than a parallel launcher.
* *Identity ladder vs the pace-identity re-gestalt* — quirt/martingale/kimberwick allocation
  must re-derive over immutable studbook-minted pace identities and the insignia glyph space.
* *Pace-state enum* — the V4 enum (green / ready / reined / candidate / complete / abandoned)
  encodes the schooled-state idea; it must re-derive against the pace model as it stands after
  the studbook cutover — as must the chain field (the slate-time inter-pace dependency
  metadata) the V4 workup planned alongside it.
* *Tackle's execution layer* — blazes, actions, exclusivity scopes, and gait affinities are the
  maximal-tackle remainder, homed in the tackle aspirant sheaf; school-time blaze resolution and
  exclusivity enforcement during concurrent dispatch are joint design between these two sheaves,
  and school walks the association graph's declared edges, not just blazes (the tackle
  aspirant sheaf's relation layer).

== Open forks (carried from the V4 workup)

* Gait record schema — beat templates, confidence gates, model preferences, required blazes;
  deferred until first gaits are built.
* Provender emission convention — how a session emits provender (structured block, tool call,
  delimiter); deferred until first gaits.
* Provender size limits — same size-guard philosophy as commits?
* Memory curation — how prior-volte context reaches school prompts.
* Longe output format and verb surface.
* Ready vs reined — is the distinction clear enough to earn two states?
* Chukker 0 semantics — always the first execution layer, or available for setup beats?
* Kimberwick exhaustion — how surfaced, what remains available.
* Failed-beat retry — re-arm in place vs new volte only.
* Beat-completion detection — how the orchestrator knows a session finished its beat (worktree
  commits, exit code, status file) and maps the result back to the beat row.
* API-key direct REST dispatch — deferred alternative to session-based dispatch.
* Command-surface visibility — how beat status, chukker progress, and provender roll up into
  show/orient; read-only provender inspection for debugging.
* Phase-boundary validation — pre-school blaze/chain checks, pre-breeze beat-map integrity
  (including nosebag expectation satisfaction), post-corral volte completion.
