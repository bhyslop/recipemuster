This is an executory operation sheaf of the {jjs0_imprimatur}: binding as design — cite it and build to it; the work product does not yet enact it.

= Dispatch and Billet

The operation family at JJ's shell edge: two doors — {jjdd_saddle} and {jjdd_lunge} — one
shared {jjdd_spine}, the {jjdd_billet} a dispatched session runs in, the {jjdd_muck} step
that clears spent billets, the {jjdd_refit} staleness remedy, and the {jjdd_cashier}.
Everything here composes {jjdf_farrier} primitives and the {jjdb_journal};
this sheaf owns no primitive of its own.

*The doors are the sole entrance*: every JJ-dispatched session arrives
through {jjdd_saddle} or {jjdd_lunge}, and the two share the one {jjdd_spine}.
A bare hand-launched session carries no MCP registration and no JJ context — it is simply a
non-JJ session, forever legitimate as such;
no fallback paths until genuinely forced.
The consequence is load-bearing, not cosmetic: the entrance must be pleasant very early —
which is what forces {jjdd_muck}'s silence when it has nothing to reap.

== The billet

[[jjdd_billet]]
//axo_entity
{jjdd_billet}::
The standing thing a dispatched session runs in: a root with a lifecycle on the partition
axis (farrier sheaf, Two axes — worktree for plain git, full clone the fallback for kinds
without worktrees), plus a branch-or-detached-tip and an env posture (the per-billet BURV
exports below).
The object noun completing the verbs-act-on-nouns grammar: mount acts on a pace,
{jjdd_saddle} creates a {jjdd_billet}, {jjdd_muck} collects it.
Boarded on arrival, kept for the duration, collected after — never by the session inside it.
+
Two kinds, split by door.
A *pace billet* ({jjdd_saddle}) seats the pace's branch — the bare {jjdt_coronet}, no glyph:
a git ref is machine context — and its dirname is `jjqb_{coronet}` per the {jjdw_yard}
charter.
A *groom billet* ({jjdd_lunge}) seats a detached checkout at trunk tip — nothing must
survive it — and its dirname is `jjqb_{firemark}`, the same signet, typed by length exactly
as identities are everywhere.
Billets are {jjdw_infield} peers of their {jjdw_hippodrome} — peer placement is load-bearing
(station geography, cosmology sheaf) — and worktrees share the hippodrome's object store:
no history duplication, no fetch between billet and hippodrome;
the billet-side cost is working files and cold build artifacts only.
+
*Billets are chat-ephemeral; branches are durable.*
The pace branch is the WIP carrier across chats, journaled in pace state;
the worktree is disposable scaffolding.
`git worktree list` is the authoritative local registry of which branch is seated where —
no worktree path is ever written into the {jjdb_studbook} (the no-worktree-paths rivet,
studbook sheaf).
+
*Every dispatched session gets its own billet; trunk belongs to the operator alone.*
Session isolation is structural, not disciplinary — even a "read-only" groom can run builds.
Git itself enforces the split: a branch checked out in one worktree cannot be checked out in
another — pace billets own unique branches and groom billets are detached, so the constraint
never binds, and a billet mechanically cannot seat the trunk branch while the
{jjdw_hippodrome} holds it.

== The doors

JJ opens the Claude Code sessions we work in.
The operator knows the verb at chat-top, and the session's {jjdd_billet} must be right at
launch — mid-session directory change neither reloads project context nor survives the cd
prohibition.
The doors are operator-typed at the shell, from within the elected clone: *cwd elects the
clone* — the pace cannot, since parallel clones of one sire are all legitimate
{jjdw_hippodrome_s} — and either door works from trunk or billet alike
({jjdf_identify} resolves either).

[[jjdd_saddle]]
{jjdd_saddle}::
The work dispatch.
A {jjdt_coronet} saddles that pace;
a {jjdt_firemark} saddles the heat's *next actionable pace* — mounting a heat has always
meant mounting its first pace, and the firemark spelling keeps that meaning.
Either way the session gets a pace billet, and the launch consumes the pace's designation
per the two-source choice (Session launch below).
The bash-layer verb is deliberately not "mount" — Unix owns that word; the two-layer split
is the codex's vocabulary-isolation doctrine at a new boundary.
+
This definition is the word's one dispatch-layer home.

[[jjdd_lunge]]
{jjdd_lunge}::
The groom dispatch: working the horse from the ground on the long line, deliberately
unmounted — assessment without riding.
A {jjdt_firemark} gets a groom billet (detached at trunk tip);
the launch takes the judgment constant (Session launch below).

[[jjdd_cashier]]
//axd_ashlar
{jjdd_cashier}::
The door that dismisses a derelict lock-holder from service:
`sight` every JJ {jjdb_blotter} lock, report the observed {jjdb_guidon_s}, then lease-guarded
`pluck` per lock, never blind.
Outside the {jjdd_spine} — no dispatch crosses it, and nothing composes it.
The door cites the {jjdb_break} per lock and adds nothing to its mechanism (journal sheaf):
what the door contributes is *deliberateness*, which is the door's property and never the
sequence's.
Precedent `/vvc-BREAK-LOCK`.
+
*Two modes.*
*Sight-and-report* is read-only and always safe — it takes nothing, breaks nothing, and is
the mode an agent or a script may run freely.
*Break* mutates, and passes the confirm gate below.
+
*The roster.* The door sights a roster of {jjdb_blotter} configs, engine-known like every
{jjdb_blotter_p} own coordinates (entity sheaf, bootstrap config) — never pedigree-resolved.
Today the roster holds one entry, the {jjdb_studbook};
the mews fleet store joins it when its contract lands, and not before — its coordinates
invented early would be a forward reference with nothing behind it.
The `vvx` commit lock (`refs/vvg/locks/vvx`, precedent above) is a *different apparatus* with
its own door and is NOT in this roster:
it guards a consumer repo's commits, not a {jjdb_blotter}, and it speaks no {jjdb_guidon}.
Stated so that a later hand does not unify two locks that share only a shape.
+
*The confirm gate*, and what it must show.
The gate is this door's contract, not the {jjdb_break_p} — a break fired without a human
answering is this door failing at the one thing it exists to do.
Per lock, the gate shows:
+
--
* the four {jjdb_guidon} fields, with acquire time rendered as an *age* — the operator judges
  staleness on age, never on a timestamp they must subtract in their head;
* a *liveness warning* that warns and never blocks.
  A ceremony runs in seconds, so a lock under a minute old is probably a live writer, not a
  corpse.
  The warning is advice to a human who may know something the clock does not (that the station
  is unplugged, that the process is gone);
  a door that refused would simply be un-runnable in the case it exists for.
* the *differentiated consequence*, per `JJr_c4e` (journal sheaf):
  breaking a live *writer* costs that writer its ceremony and nothing more — the lease
  refuses its `consign` and *nothing lands*;
  breaking a live *acting reader* is the accepted exposure — it may act on a stale image, and
  nothing catches it.
  The two are not the same risk, and a gate that collapsed them would teach the operator the
  wrong thing about their own decision;
* *which store* the lock guards — a roster of one today, and a wrong-store break tomorrow is
  exactly the mistake a one-line report invites.
--
+
*Discoverability.* A stranded lock surfaces to the operator as a `LockHeld` refusal — at
every {jjdd_muck}, hence at every dispatch, once muck is live.
That refusal text must NAME this door.
A cure the sufferer cannot find is not a cure, and the refusal is the only moment the
operator is guaranteed to be looking.

== The entrance spine

[[jjdd_spine]]
{jjdd_spine}::
The one shared sequence behind both doors — a door contributes only its target typing and
its tier source, never its own spine:
+
. *{jjdd_muck}* — the billet-clearing sweep, silent when its reap set is empty.
. *{jjdf_identify}* at the captured invocation path — the door captures cwd exactly once;
  a decline is the fair-faced foreign-ground rejection.
. *Pedigree lookup* — a {jjdb_studbook} read, no lock, one indirection:
  derived key → sire → {jjdb_pedigree};
  a key resolving no sire is the unrecorded-sire rejection, and a claiming
  kind that contradicts the recorded kind is the record/ground-drift rejection
  ({jjdf_identify} contract, farrier sheaf).
. *Billet ensure* — `billet_create`-or-reuse ({jjdf_billet} facet);
  a groom billet in reuse re-detaches to trunk tip.
. *`glean`* — the spine fetches and never merges;
  staleness becomes known here so the open can report it, and {jjdd_refit} is the remedy.
. *BURV export* — the per-billet env posture (BUK meld below).
. *Provision* — JJ context and MCP registration, session-scoped (Launch-time provisioning
  below).
. *Launch* — start Claude Code inside the {jjdd_billet} with the engagement verb as opening
  prompt, at the (tier, effort) the two-source choice yields (Session launch below).
  The interior launch primitive is stirrup — hearting, pace-blind, parameterized (billet,
  tier, opening prompt); pace-coupling lives in the caller, and later dispatch surfaces
  reuse the same spine and primitive, never a parallel launcher.

*Report precedes launch.*
The door's report introduces the session it launches, so it reaches the operator's console
BEFORE the Launch step hands over the terminal — the whole resolved report (billet, launch
tier and effort, opening prompt, and any staleness notice), never trailing the session's exit.
The console-handoff is the spine's last act for exactly this reason: a report emitted after it
would land beneath the very session it was meant to precede.

Mount absorbs cold start — billet ensure, glean, orient are the spine's work, and no
separate cold-start engagement verb exists.

== Muck

[[jjdd_muck]]
{jjdd_muck}::
Mucking out the stable: the reap of spent {jjdd_billet_s}, homed as the {jjdd_spine_p}
leading step — run implicitly on both doors, never directly invoked, no door of its own.
Reaping is nuke-on-start: teardown belongs to the next dispatch, never to the session whose
floor a billet is.
+
The reap set: pace billets whose pace is closed and whose age exceeds retention;
groom billets past retention (they carry nothing durable).
The liveness guard is the pure JJ-data join — live {jjdxo_officium_s} × billets — a platform
process-probe only ever belt-and-braces;
the retention window equals the {jjdxo_exsanguination} window — one constant, one rhyme.
The sweep globs `jjqb_*` ({jjdw_yard}), a positive match that structurally excludes the kit
and record repos it must never reap.
+
Behavior: snapshot pace states through the journal sheaf's read bracket — the snapshot arms
the reap, so it is an acting read, never a reporting one ({jjdk_lockless_reads} scope rule):
lock, advance, read, release, and what it reads is the store's truth at that moment.
Then plan, confirm, and reap lock-free.
The bracket's accepted costs land here on the entrance path:
a sweep may be refused LockHeld while a stranger is mid-write (it reports and yields — the
next dispatch sweeps),
and a crashed sweep strands a stale lock for the {jjdb_break} like any other bracket holder.
Plan-then-confirm when the reap set is non-empty;
*silent when empty* — the sole-entrance consequence above forces this.
On a dirty billet, refuse-with-advice by default;
auto-commit-push rides behind an explicit confirm — never silently.
+
Deliberately sacrificed for MVP: immediate reap of a not-yet-closed billet.
Such a billet waits for pace-close plus retention, and the next dispatch takes it.

== Refit

[[jjdd_refit]]
{jjdd_refit}::
The session-facing staleness remedy: when the ground moves, refit.
No verb refuses on a stale trunk — staleness is drift, never corruption (the billet branch
is additive and the {jjdb_studbook} advances under its own lock) — so {jjdo_open} leads its
report with the staleness warning and names {jjdd_refit} as the remedy, and notch and wrap
append the same recommendation whenever trunk has moved.
+
Behavior: `enfold` trunk into the billet branch — merge, never rebase: journaled
{jjdb_counterfoil_s} survive, and the branch's interior history dies at the operator's trunk
merge anyway — then `consign` the merge immediately: the operation exists to clear staleness
for every station and runs online by construction;
offline degrades to warn-and-proceed.
On conflict the attended session resolves as its first order of business — *attended* is the
load-bearing property, not tier;
{jjdd_saddle} may bump the tier when it detects a pending conflict, but tier-escalation
machinery is deferred past the MVP (breeze-era).

== Session launch

The tier vocabulary is the vendor's own family words — a Palisade admission at one membrane,
never a JJ mint (the {jjdf_palisade} posture applied to model families).
No tier categories are hardlined: a tier is just a name, and category judgment lives at each
policy site as an explicit name-set, never as an attribute a family IS.
Effort anchors transparently on the vendor's effort classification, likewise unminted;
effort never rides the MCP wire — it is designation-and-dispatch data, and the orient guard
reads tier alone.

One roster table, baked into the engine as a constant (the {jjdb_blotter_p} engine-known
posture): family name → launch model ID + valid effort set, no default columns.
Every kind of vendor drift lands as an edit to this one table, and a surprise is captured as
a spook.
Stirrup is the table's one launch consumer — callers speak tier words, never model IDs, and
an invalid (family, effort) pair refuses fair-facedly.
The fable family stays a roster row no launch policy names until its pricing settles;
opus is the working judgment tier.

The (tier, effort) choice has exactly two sources, split by door:

* {jjdd_lunge}, having no pace, launches the *judgment constant* — opus/xhigh, one named
  cell in the engine.
* {jjdd_saddle} launches the pace's designation exactly;
  on an unbridled pace it launches the same judgment constant — undesignated work is
  judgment work: such a session plans toward a bridle or simply finishes fast.
  A designation without effort launches with the effort knob omitted — the vendor default
  governs; JJ invents nothing.

Guard permanence: every command registers its guard bucket explicitly, no default — absence
fails loud.
The docket-authoring gate is an explicit accept-set of family names beside the roster;
an unknown family sits in no accept-set by construction — OPEN reads only, until a
deliberate roster-and-set edit.

Programmatic session dispatch for personal tooling is documented product surface (headless
mode, Agent SDK, `--model`);
recording that standing determination here is what keeps it from being re-litigated from
memory.

== The BUK meld

BUK dispatch self-anchors — cwd normalizes to the invoked tree's root, and moorings ride
committed in any worktree — so BUK cannot tell a {jjdd_billet} from a clone, and the station
family already resolves to the {jjdw_infield} (peer placement load-bearing, cosmology
sheaf).
The genuine hazard is concurrency, not geometry: shared per-dispatch output state
(`../output-buk/current/`, `logs-buk/last.txt`).
The {jjdd_spine} closes it by exporting per-billet `BURV_OUTPUT_ROOT_DIR`,
`BURV_TEMP_ROOT_DIR`, and `BURV_LOG_DIR` — absolute paths, the whoever-dispatches-owns-the-knob
protocol; theurge needs no change (it sets BURV innermost, and innermost wins).
Named behavior change: chain facts (`current/` → `previous/`) become *billet-scoped* — a
tabtarget inside the billet and one in a trunk terminal no longer see each other's flow,
which also closes a latent cross-clone chain race that predates billets.

== The trampolines

The shell doors are {jjdw_infield}-resident trampolines under the `jjy_` signet
({jjdw_yard}): `jjy_saddle` and `jjy_lunge`, two-line scripts — never symlinks: Windows
stations — that exec into the kit repo's tabtargets.
They are stamped by an idempotent kit-repo installer verb at founding — re-running it is
always safe, and its boring hearting name mints at implementation.
Nothing ever lands in a {jjdw_hippodrome}, and no gitignore is needed anywhere — the
{jjdw_infield} is not a repository.

== Launch-time provisioning

{jjdw_hippodrome_s} carry no JJ inserts (cosmology sheaf), so MCP config, the verb table,
and JJ context arrive *at session launch from the spine* — session-scoped config plus the
opening prompt — never from repo-resident inserts;
the project's own CLAUDE.md, whatever the vanilla project ships, still governs its code.

The destination is generated, work-scoped context: context honed
to the elected heat or pace — acronym rows the {jjdl_tackle} bindings name, the verb table
cut to the session type.
Default stance until that intelligence exists: retain everything the current inserts carry,
removing pieces only as honing is actually built.
Honing is prediction, and starvation fails silent — the full table is also the index of what
exists — so two standing repairs ship with every provision: an invariant *conduct core*,
unhoned (never-reach-past, commit discipline, additive-only), and a *pull door* — a
context-lookup verb named in the core — turning starvation into one extra round-trip instead
of silent ignorance.
Selection is safe only when paired with discoverability.

Per-scope discipline context arrives through the sire's {jjdl_tackle} bindings, read from
the {jjdb_pedigree} at this point in the spine (tackle sheaf);
the two repairs above are that sheaf's consumer obligations, stated there.
Officium exchange (gazettes) relocates into the {jjdb_studbook} clone's untracked working
area — a forward reference to its aspirant home (state-repo sheaf), landing with it.

== Stirruped entry

A dispatched session arrives with the engagement verb in its opening prompt, so target
election shifts form: the door already elected the pace or heat, and the opening prompt
carries it — the hand-opened session's target-election dance is not performed.
A session saddled onto a pace bridled at a *sub-frontier* tier follows the designee protocol
(orient, work, record, landing — never wrap);
a judgment session — {jjdd_lunge}, {jjdd_saddle} on an unbridled pace, or {jjdd_saddle} on a
pace bridled at a *frontier* tier — keeps the full ceremony.
A frontier designation names which tier may act, never that the acting session is a designee:
demoting it would make the frontier tiers undesignable in practice, forcing judgment work to
park in an unbridled pace and stripping the designation of the one thing it records.
The kit-core protocol texts pick this seam up at implementation.

== Rejections

The {jjdd_spine_p} refusals are fair-faced ashlar output, named per the farrier taxonomy
where a primitive supplies them:
foreign ground ({jjdf_identify} declines — no kind claims the tree),
unrecorded sire (the claimed key resolves no sire),
record/ground drift (claiming kind contradicts the recorded kind),
and {jjdd_muck_p} dirty-anomaly refusal (refuse-with-advice, above).
Everything else fails loud through the composed primitive's own named rejection kind.
