= Studbook

The regional record repo: the {jjdb_blotter_p} first instance, and the physical vessel of the
{jjdgr_gallops}.
Store mechanics — the lock ref and its {jjdb_guidon}, the write bracket, the break sequence —
are the {jjdb_blotter_p} contract and the journal sheaf's sequences;
this sheaf homes the tenant: what the {jjdb_studbook} holds, and how its entries know the work
they record.

== The entity

[[jjdb_studbook]]
//axo_entity
{jjdb_studbook}::
The regional record repo — one {jjdb_blotter} serving every station and every jockeyed
project.
The {jjdgr_gallops} stays the live state-concept;
the {jjdb_studbook} is the physical vessel that holds it.
As a {jjdb_blotter} it is linear, single-writer-under-lock, and engine-driven:
every write passes through the {jjdb_journal} ({jjdk_sole_door}),
reads take no lock ({jjdk_lockless_reads}),
and its own coordinates are engine-known, never pedigree-resolved.

== Pedigree

[[jjdb_pedigree]]
{jjdb_pedigree}::
The per-sire record: one {jjdb_pedigree} per *sire* — the registered external body of work
JJ serves, a transport-independent identity (a git clone today, perhaps a tarball someday
under another driver kind) — stored in the {jjdb_studbook} — the {jjdb_studbook} is a
register of {jjdb_pedigree_s}.
The {jjdb_pedigree} keys on the sire's addresses directly.
The registered-identity indirection — a derived key resolving to a minted sire id, the
{jjdb_pedigree} keyed on that id rather than the address — is deliberately not carried: no
consumer resolves it, so the field would seed durable committed records with a value nothing
sets meaningfully (`jjrds_Pedigree` doc comment, journal backbone). It lands as an optional field
the day address mobility is real.
A new sire needs a founding {jjdb_pedigree} entry before JJ serves it — a deliberate
ceremony;
the derivation chain that consumes the entry is world model, homed in the cosmology sheaf.

Each {jjdb_pedigree} carries:

* the {jjdf_farrier} kind — which revision-control driver serves clones of this sire —
  and its parameters (trunk name);
* the sire's tackle set — its filesets and the governance each carries, and in time their
  declared interrelations — as a sibling entry ({jjdl_tackle}, tackle sheaf).
  The {jjdl_bounds} seat in the tackle table itself, never here: coverage is tackle
  machinery, and a second home would be a second truth;
* the {jjdd_livery} path prefix, org-demand-only and absent in the ordinary case — the
  posture and its home are the dispatch sheaf's ({jjdd_livery}, dispatch sheaf);
* reserved, declared but not carried: the {jjdb_footprint} posture (Footprint, below).
  No consumer resolves it, so carrying it would seed durable committed records with a value
  nothing sets meaningfully;
  it lands as an optional field the day a consumer is real — a non-breaking add, on the same
  deferral the address-mobility attribute rides;
* reserved, parked: a per-sire edit-scope wildcard list, if billets-per-pace ever prove
  insufficient for collision avoidance.

== Footprint

What JJ leaves behind in a sire is partitioned by ownership, never by convenience.

*Work products* — commits and branches, the code changes themselves — are the registered
body's property.
They reach the sire in the form its owner tolerates, and that form is the
{jjdb_pedigree_p} to name, never the {jjdw_engine_p} to assume.
*Knowledge products* — the {jjdgr_gallops}, {jjdb_counterfoil_s}, captured session
transcripts, guides, and grimoire — are JJ's own, and never land in a sire at all:
they live in the {jjdb_studbook} and the {jjdw_infield}.
This partition is what lets a {jjdw_hippodrome} stay vanilla;
it is the boundary "What never enters the record" draws, read from the other side.

The proposition is a rivet, cited in shipped code by the opaque token `JJr_f30`:
its identifier carries no meaning, so it leaks none into the rust that ships without this
veiled spec, and one `grep JJr_f30` returns this definition and every site it guards.
The {jjdd_muck} door's JJ-owned-prefix filter is a citation site — its salvage arm refuses
to lodge JJ's own {jjdxo_exchange} into a work repo even on a dirty, confirmed billet.

[[jjdb_footprint]]
{jjdb_footprint}::
The per-{jjdb_pedigree} posture governing the form in which work products reach their sire —
the axis the narrower *remotes posture* named, before the partition above was seen to be the
thing being parameterized.
+
*Declared, not carried.*
No {jjdb_pedigree} record holds a footprint field, and nothing reads one:
the default arm below is simply what the machinery does, unconditionally.
This entry names the axis and fixes its arms so the field is a non-breaking add whenever a
consumer earns it;
until then a reader must not expect to find a footprint in the record.
+
Two arms stand:
+
* *default* — personal branches: JJ pushes branches of its own to the sire, and the body's
  owner takes the work from there;
* *constrained* — one personal branch carrying a prepared patch series (format-patch
  lineage), for a body whose owner tolerates no more than that.
+
The constrained arm is spoken by a {jjdf_farrier} kind of its own rather than by a flag on
the default one: how a series is prepared and offered is driver behaviour, and a kind is
where driver behaviour lives.
+
Whichever arm a {jjdb_pedigree} names, the partition is unconditional —
no posture makes a knowledge product deliverable.

== Sojourn

[[jjdk_sojourn]]
//axk_premise
{jjdk_sojourn}::
A {jjdb_pedigree} records a bounded stay, not a possession:
JJ arrives at a sire, delivers traction on it, and departs.
The bodies of work outlive JJ; JJ's claim on any of them ends when the work does.
The world model already speaks this from the ground's side — JJ visits a {jjdw_hippodrome},
never moves in — and this premise is that stance read from the record's side.
+
Consequently a {jjdb_pedigree} entry *concludes by status, never by deletion*.
The {jjdb_studbook} is a register, and a register that erases its concluded lines stops
being a record of what was served.
+
A disengagement ceremony — retiring a footprint per the posture the {jjdb_pedigree} names —
is out of scope at MVP.
What the premise binds now is that concluding a stay is a status transition, and that
nothing left standing in the sire is JJ's to reclaim.

== Counterfoil

[[jjdb_counterfoil]]
{jjdb_counterfoil}::
The retained stub every journaled entry carries, naming the issued work:
a member→SHA manifest — one-element for a single repo, generalizing unchanged to submodule
and multi-repo constellations — annotated with branch and a dirty flag.
The reference is deliberately one-way:
the {jjdb_studbook} points at the {jjdw_hippodrome}, never the reverse.
The only hippodrome-side mark is the session-id commit trailer,
a denormalized cache justified by query convenience, never by recoverability.

*Rebase soft spot (known, accepted):*
a rebase after journaling rewrites SHAs and dangles the {jjdb_counterfoil} —
degraded provenance, not corruption; nothing downstream builds on it.
Convergence discipline already prefers merge over rebase, which preserves
{jjdb_counterfoil_s};
{jjdd_billet} branches never rebase ({jjdd_refit} composes the merge-based `enfold`, a
{jjdf_billet} op), closing the gap structurally where {jjdd_billet_s} are used.
An optional `counterfoil_verify` op ({jjdf_farrier} op census, deferred past MVP) turns a
dangling {jjdb_counterfoil} from a silent lie into a flagged one.

== What never enters the record

The {jjdb_studbook} is a regional record; a worktree path is a station-local fact.
*No worktree path is ever written into the {jjdb_studbook}* —
issued work is named by member, SHA, and branch, never by filesystem location.
The proposition classifies as a rivet;
its opaque tail mints at implementation, citing this section.

== Journal entries as commits

Every {jjdgr_gallops} mutation is one {jjdb_journal} bracket, so every mutation *is* a git
commit.
The commit message may carry the plan in gazette notice format as a review surface for
`git log`;
the JSON in the {jjdgr_gallops} is truth, the message a denormalized presentation.

== Scope at birth

First tenant: the {jjdgr_gallops} only.
Captured chat histories and action-choices are the *intended second tenant*, deferred;
their contract — including the chat↔commit correlation — stays aspirant with them
(`JJSAS-state-repo.adoc`).
SUPERSEDED AFTER CUTOVER (operator ruling 260722, paddock-tenancy repair): the
gallops-alone scope did not survive contact with the de-insertion sweep (₢B3·CAAAT),
which emptied the consumer-repo `.claude/jjm/` home the paddock subsystem still read —
the two landed rulings collided, and every paddock consumer broke.
The operator ruled the JJ state tenants into the studbook: heat paddocks (`paddocks/`,
case-safe `jjp_` filenames unchanged), retire trophies (`retired/`), and the operator
files `jji_itch.md` / `jjz_scar.md` (studbook root, kit-unread), seated by migration
commit e0d4f73 from the pre-sweep tree.
Tenant writes ride the mutation's own {jjdb_journal} bracket (a curry, nominate, or
retire commits its paddock/trophy beside the gallops in one ₶ commit); work code stays
on the consumer-repo path.
The deferred chat-history second tenant is untouched by this ruling.

== Officium exchange and record

The {jjdb_studbook}'s local clone hosts more than its committed tenants above: a fixed,
untracked subdir (one per officium) co-locates two things that never enter the record until
they graduate — {jjdxo_exchange} (gazettes: wire format, single-call lifetime, forever
untracked) and the officium's *record* half (identity metadata — station,
hippodrome-or-billet, session, per {jjdf_identify}'s officium-open composition,
`JJSVF-farrier.adoc` "Toothing: officium open" — and counterfoils, eventually the captured
session transcripts of this section's second tenant).
Co-locate now, commit later: graduating the record half to committed history is a
gitignore-line change, never a relocation — the studbook already knows this one location on
every station.
Because graduation is that small, it forces no schema review of its own: what may enter the
record binds here, before the record is ever written.
The {jjdw_hippodrome}-or-{jjdd_billet} member enters as the seat's role reading alone — a
`Partition` carries its primary's root, and that root is a station-local worktree path, barred
by "What never enters the record" above (the rivet whose tail mints when the record write
lands).
+
This supersedes {jjdxo_officium}'s former curia-resident exchange (JJS0 Officium Lifecycle,
Directory Layout): the enablement seam (`jjrm_mcp.rs`,
`JJRM_OFFICIUM_STUDBOOK_ENABLED`) flipped at the conversion heat (JJSAS Founding-and-cutover),
and {jjdxo_exchange} now lives here.
