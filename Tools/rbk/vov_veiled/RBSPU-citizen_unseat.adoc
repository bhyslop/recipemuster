//axhob_operation
{rbtgo_citizen_unseat}

NOTE: This operation withdraws one
{rbtf_mantle}
from a
{rbtf_citizen}
in the
{rbtgi_depot}
— the
{rbtf_unseat}
act, the everyday removal verb and the structural inverse of
{rbtf_brevet}.
It withdraws the muniment, then removes only the `tokenCreator` binding on the mantle
{giam_service_account};
the depot-scoped `serviceUsageConsumer` binding is left in place. A citizen unseated of
every mantle is therefore _suspended_, not erased — visible, recoverable, and cheap to
re-admit with a fresh
{rbtf_brevet}.
{rbtf_attaint}
alone sweeps the depot-scoped binding.

NOTE: The
{rbtr_governor}
performs the unseat, wielding the donned governor
{rbtf_mantle}.
The citizen's federated workforce subject arrives as the param1 folio; the mantle to
remove is the second positional argument (`governor` | `director` | `retriever`). The
composition body is token-agnostic — the shared core the donned verb, the
founding-exception path, and the interim proof all drive.

NOTE: *Intent-first ordering.* The
{rbtf_muniment}
is withdrawn before the binding is removed. A crash after the muniment withdraw but
before the revoke leaves a visible surplus — a live binding without its recorded
holding — which the read-only reconciliation diff surfaces and a re-run of unseat
clears; never a silent grant a rehearsal would miss.

NOTE: *One canonical revoke path.* Unseat removes the `principal://` member from the
`RBGC_ROLE_IAM_SERVICE_ACCOUNT_TOKEN_CREATOR` binding on the mantle
{giam_service_account}
via the SA-scope principal revoke — the inverse of the brevet SA-scope principal grant,
the same distinct canonical path (never a normalizer over the `serviceAccount:`
siblings). The depot `serviceUsageConsumer` binding is deliberately untouched: that is
the suspension contract.
