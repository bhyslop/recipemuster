//axhob_operation
{rbtgo_citizen_attaint}

NOTE: This operation expels a
{rbtf_citizen}
wholly from the
{rbtgi_depot}
— the
{rbtf_attaint}
act, the rare whole-person expulsion (attainder: the old law's civic death). It
unseats the subject from every
{rbtf_mantle}
the citizen could hold, then sweeps the depot-scoped `serviceUsageConsumer` binding
that
{rbtf_unseat}
deliberately leaves behind, then notes the deregistration. Removal of the principal on
the IdP side remains the IdP administrator's act, out of scope here.

NOTE: The
{rbtr_governor}
performs the attaint, wielding the donned governor
{rbtf_mantle}.
The citizen's federated workforce subject arrives as the param1 folio; attaint takes no
mantle argument — it acts across all mantles. The composition body is token-agnostic;
the interim admission proof drives the same core payor-credentialed.

NOTE: *Composed of unseat plus the sweep.* Attaint is definitionally
{rbtf_unseat}-all
followed by the depot-scoped sweep — the only verb that removes the
`serviceUsageConsumer` binding. Unseating an unheld mantle is an idempotent no-op
(absent muniment, absent binding), so the across-all-mantles loop is safe whatever the
citizen actually held.

NOTE: *Crash leaves surplus, never resurrection.* The teardown runs muniment-then-binding
per mantle, then the sweep. A crash mid-teardown leaves a partial expulsion as visible
surplus — some bindings still live without their muniments — which the read-only
reconciliation diff surfaces and a re-run clears. No partial path re-grants anything.
