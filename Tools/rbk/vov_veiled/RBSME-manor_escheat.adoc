{rbtgo_manor_escheat}

NOTE: This operation escheats the
{rbtf_terrier}
— the manor-hygiene sweep that strikes from the record what no live admission path
owns, reverting ownerless holdings to the
{rbtr_payor}
as an escheat reverts a dead tenant's holding to the lord. Two stray classes
accrete: the *orphaned polity slice* — unmaking a depot project
({rbtgo_depot_unmake})
never sweeps the payor-grain terrier, so a retired levy's slice (its
{rbtf_muniment_s}
and its polity managed folder) persists — and the *dead-schema stray* — an object
that fails the current muniment contract (RBSTN), stranded by schema churn where
the admission verbs cannot
{rbtf_expunge}
it.
{rbtf_rehearse}
witnesses both (its depot-attributed roll) but mutates nothing: rehearse stays a
pure read, and the escheat is the separate mutating act — the cinched split.

NOTE: *Payor-credentialed, of necessity.* Striking objects across polity slices
exceeds any governor
{rbtf_mantle}'s
folder-scoped write, and an orphaned slice has no living governor mantle at all —
its SA died with the depot project. The
{rbtr_payor}
acts as owner of the
{rbtgi_payor_project}
the terrier bucket lives in. The operation accepts no CLI parameters.

NOTE: *Depot-liveness rule.* A polity slice is judged by its depot project:
`ACTIVE` bearing the depot displayName anchor is *live* (kept); `DELETE_REQUESTED`,
`403`, and `404` are *dead* (the slice is swept). An unmake is deliberate
retirement — waiting out the ~30-day purge window would leave every freshly-unmade
slice standing and make the sweep unexercisable — and an undeleted depot re-admits
cheaply through the idempotent admission verbs, so sweeping at `DELETE_REQUESTED`
loses nothing durable. The conservative residue: an `ACTIVE` project *without* the
depot anchor is an anomaly — flagged in the plan and *kept*, since the sweep
removes only what is provably dead or provably malformed.

NOTE: *Plan-then-confirm, idempotent.* The sweep enumerates first and shows its
full plan — every keep, every strike, with the reason — and only then demands
confirmation (the operator types the terrier bucket name; the standard
confirm-skip seam suppresses the prompt for non-interactive runs). The plan *is*
the confirmation subject, which is why the confirm sits after the survey rather
than leading zero-traffic as
{rbtgo_manor_raze}'s
does — nothing mutates before the gate. A clean terrier short-circuits: nothing
sweepable is reported as already-clean and the operation exits `0` without
prompting, so the escheat is an idempotent no-op on a healthy manor.

NOTE: *Scope boundary.* The escheat reaches the terrier alone: bucket objects and
the polity managed folders of dead depots. No IAM binding is touched (a dead
depot's bindings died with its project; a live depot's belong to the admission
verbs), no workforce pool or provider is read or written, and the bucket itself
stands. Provider-liveness is deliberately out of scope: a
{rbtf_muniment}
whose admitting provider was dissolved by
{rbtf_jilt}
stands — jilt/re-affiance churn must not erase admission records; only a dead
depot or a dead schema condemns an object.

NOTE: *Raw-grain data layer, beneath the muniment contract.* The escheat's two
data-layer sub-operations — the classifying *survey* and the *raw expunge* —
deliberately operate at the raw bucket-object grain, beneath the muniment
sub-operations of the access contract (RBSTR), because their subjects are
precisely the objects that fail or predate that contract. The survey classifies
each object against the current muniment shape (RBSTN): the three-segment key
`«depot»/«mantle»/«subject»` with the mantle in its enum, a JSON body
carrying the two non-empty `rbgft_` fields, and body-key agreement on
(mantle, subject). Body-key agreement is what condemns a retired key schema: the
subject reads as the key's remainder, so a key carrying extra segments (the
pre-drop four-segment provider key) disagrees with its own body and classifies
as a dead-schema stray. A read-after-list `404` is the benign vanish, skipped
exactly as the peruse lister skips it. The raw expunge deletes by listed object
name unconditioned (`204` deleted, `404` already-absent — both clean). Their
deliberate rejections share the escheat band: one gate, its deficits rules within
it, distinct from the engross/expunge/peruse bands whose verbs may co-occur with
an escheat in a spawn path.

NOTE: *Proof status — both arms live-proven.* The dead-schema arm is live-proven
(260702: a real provider-blind stray swept from the standing terrier, verify
re-survey green, second run idempotent-clean). The orphaned-slice arm is
live-proven (260711: twelve depots left dead by a real levy churn, each judged
`DELETE_REQUESTED` by the liveness probe and struck as fifteen objects and eleven
managed folders, while the standing depot's slice was kept whole; verify re-survey
green; a second run short-circuited clean without reaching the confirm). The churn
stranded both partial shapes a levy can leave — a slice bearing a muniment whose
managed folder was never created, and folders bearing no muniment at all — and the
depot set, taken as the union over objects and managed folders, reached each. Two
verdicts of the depot-liveness rule remain unexercised against a real slice: the
`403` and `404` dead branches have only ever been reasoned, never witnessed. They
are proven at whatever churn produces them, never by a synthetic payor-direct
writer seeding a fake slice — that scaffold shape is retired (operator ruling at
the 260702 exercise).

. *Survey the
{rbtf_terrier}*:
.. {rbbc_call}
the terrier bucket's object listing (paged) and each object's body; classify every
object against the muniment contract — verdict *sound* (with its depot segment) or
*stray* (with the deficit: key shape, mantle enum, body not JSON, missing
`rbgft_` fields, or body-key mismatch)
.. {rbbc_call}
the bucket's managed-folder listing (paged) —
{rbbc_store}
`«FOLDER_SET»`, each folder naming the depot of a standing polity slice
.. a read-after-list `404` on any object is skipped (benign vanish), not fatal
.. an absent terrier bucket is
{rbbc_fatal}
naming the manor-setup finisher
({rbtgo_manor_instaurate})
— the escheat presumes an instaurated manor

//axhos_step
. *Probe depot liveness*:
.. For each distinct depot named by a sound muniment or a standing folder,
{rbbc_call}
link:https://cloud.google.com/resource-manager/reference/rest/v3/projects/get[projects.get]
.. {rbbc_store}
the verdict per depot: `200` + `ACTIVE` + displayName anchor → *live*; `200` +
`ACTIVE` without the anchor → *anomaly* (kept, flagged); `200` + any other state,
`403`, or `404` → *dead*
.. any other HTTP outcome is
{rbbc_fatal}
— a liveness verdict is never guessed

//axhos_step
. *Show the plan, then gate*:
.. {rbbc_show}
every disposition: each kept muniment (live depot, sound shape), each flagged
anomaly, each planned strike with its reason (orphaned slice naming the dead
depot's state, or dead-schema deficit), and each dead-depot managed folder
planned for removal
.. *Already-clean short-circuit* — when the plan holds no strike, report the
terrier clean and exit `0` (idempotent no-op, no prompt)
.. {rbbc_require}
operator confirmation matching the terrier bucket name; the standard confirm-skip
seam suppresses the interactive prompt for non-interactive runs. Nothing mutates
before this gate.

//axhos_step
. *Execute the sweep*:
.. For each planned strike, raw-expunge the object by its listed name (`204`
deleted or `404` already-vanished — both clean; any other outcome rejects in the
escheat band)
.. For each dead depot's managed folder, purge it (any straggler objects, then the
folder itself; an absent folder is tolerated — a scaffold-era slice may predate
managed folders)

//axhos_step
. *Verify*:
.. Re-run the survey and re-apply the held liveness verdicts;
{rbbc_require}
the re-plan holds no strike — every standing object is a sound muniment under a
live (or flagged-anomaly) depot, and no dead depot's folder stands

.. {rbbc_show}
the swept counts (objects struck by class, folders removed) and the standing
remainder — how many sound muniments stand, under which live depots
