== Terrier noun

The terrier's *resource* internals are settled (at the terrier-provision pace):
the physical bucket lives in the Manor's own {rbtgi_payor_project}, its id suffixed `-terrier` — manor-grain, one per Manor — homed in the `RBGP_TERRIER_BUCKET` kindle constant and created with Uniform Bucket-Level Access enabled (managed folders require it).
The managed-folder grain is one managed folder per polity, keyed by the depot project id.
A governor mantle's write is scoped to its own polity's folder (`roles/storage.objectAdmin` granted through managed-folder IAM); read is bucket-level for every governor mantle (`roles/storage.objectViewer` on the bucket) — write folder-scoped own-polity, read manor-wide.
The payor reads inherently as owner of the payor project.
The cross-project shape — a payor-project bucket written by a depot's governor mantle — is provisioned payor-credentialed as founding-time acts at each grain:
the bucket by the manor-setup finisher (instaurate, RBSMS);
the per-polity folder and its grain IAM by the depot levy (RBSMF), beside the governor mantle it empowers.

The terrier's *muniment* internals are settled (at the terrier sub-operations pace, re-cut to grant grain under the one-pool model):
granularity is per-entry — one object per (principal subject,
{rbtf_mantle}
held) pair — so a holding is recorded by a muniment's presence and erased by its absence.
The object name indexes the pair under the polity managed folder as `«depot-project-id»/«mantle»/«principal-subject»` — the depot leads (the managed-folder grain) and the raw principal subject trails (it alone may carry slashes);
the object content is the authoritative muniment JSON, `{ rbgft_subject, rbgft_mantle }` under the `rbgft_` sprue, and the holding is read from that content — the object name is only the index.
Per-entry muniments are immutable, so writes are create-and-delete only: the generation-conditional update path of the access contract is unexercised under this granularity.

The muniment deliberately carries no provider dimension — its grain is the grant's grain:
the grantable principal names the manor's one pool and the subject, never the provider that asserted it (the brevet contract's pool-scoped member),
so two co-resident foedera admitting the same subject onto the same mantle hold the *same* binding — one grant, one muniment;
a provider segment would discriminate records the grant does not distinguish, and the byte-identical collision of a second engross (idempotent success) is the accurate one-record-per-grant mirror.
Nor is the provider derivable from the record:
co-trusted production IdPs assert one canonical subject (the brevet contract's co-tenancy rule), so the admitting provider is not a function of the subject at all.
A manor-wide
{rbtf_rehearse}
therefore attributes each holding to its polity slice (the depot key segment), never to an admitting
{rbtf_foedus}.

Its access sub-operations (
{rbtf_engross},
{rbtf_expunge},
{rbtf_peruse})
are homed in the Terrier access subdoc below.
