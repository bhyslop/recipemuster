//axhob_operation
{rbtgo_manor_raze}

NOTE: This operation force-deletes a
{rbtgi_manor}'s
one workforce identity pool — the manor-teardown, clearing the ground so a release
ladder starts from a clean manor. It is the deliberate destructive inverse of the
manor-setup finisher
({rbtgo_manor_found}):
where the finisher founds the pool and keeps it, raze tears it down. Deleting the pool
cascades to every provider hanging beneath it and ends federated access for *every*
{rbtf_citizen}
under the manor at once — a manor-wide break, not the single-foedus dissolution
{rbtf_jilt}
performs.

NOTE: *Distinct from
{rbtf_jilt},
and deliberately so.* Jilt is the everyday provider-delete — dissolve one foedus while
the shared pool stands
({rbtgo_manor_jilt}).
Raze is the rare, dangerous pool-delete. The danger lives in this separate,
scary-gated verb precisely so the everyday
{rbtf_jilt}
can never nuke the pool: tearing down the pool is never a side effect of dissolving a
foedus. Raze is release-ladder infrastructure, withheld from the delivered tabtarget
surface (the verb ships; the one-keystroke accelerator does not).

NOTE: Raze is *idempotent*. A pool that never existed or was already deleted (the probe
returns `404`), or one already soft-deleted (HTTP `200` with `state: DELETED`), is
reported already-razed and the operation exits clean without issuing a delete. Only a
live pool is deleted.

NOTE: *Payor-credentialed, pool-only.* The
{rbtr_payor}
performs the raze, reading the pool coordinates
({rbrw_org_id},
{rbrw_workforce_pool_id})
from the
{rbrw_regime}
alone — no
{rbrf_regime}
provider field, since the delete cascades the providers rather than naming one. The
operation accepts no CLI parameters.

NOTE: Scope boundary. Raze deletes exactly the manor's one workforce pool and lets its
providers cascade. The org-level admin grant, the
{rbtf_terrier}
bucket and its folders, the depot projects and their
{rbtf_mantle}
service accounts are untouched — those belong to the finisher, the levy, and their own
teardowns
({rbtgo_depot_unmake}
for the depot). Raze is the finisher's inverse on the pool axis only.

//axhos_step
. *Safety confirmation* (zero traffic — the deliberate pause precedes authentication):
.. {rbbc_require}
operator confirmation matching the pool id
{rbrw_workforce_pool_id}.
The warning names the manor-wide break: deleting the pool re-enrolls EVERY citizen under
the manor. The standard confirm-skip seam suppresses the interactive prompt for
non-interactive test runs.

//axhos_step
. *Authenticate as
{rbtr_payor}*:
.. Execute
{rbtoe_payor_authenticate}
.. {rbbc_require}
authentication successful

//axhos_step
. *Probe the pool*:
.. {rbbc_call}
link:https://cloud.google.com/iam/docs/reference/rest/v1/locations.workforcePools/get[workforcePools.get] for
{rbrw_workforce_pool_id}
under `organizations/RBRW_ORG_ID`.
.. {rbbc_store}
`«POOL_CODE»` (HTTP status) and, on `200`, the pool `state`.
.. *Already-razed short-circuit* — exit clean (idempotent no-op), no delete issued, when `«POOL_CODE»` is `404` (no pool of this id) *or* `200` with `state: DELETED` (already soft-deleted).
.. Any other non-`200` is
{rbbc_fatal}.
.. A live pool (`200`, `state` not `DELETED`) advances to deletion.

//axhos_step
. *Delete the pool*:
.. {rbbc_submit}
link:https://cloud.google.com/iam/docs/reference/rest/v1/locations.workforcePools/delete[workforcePools.delete] for the pool resource name.
.. {rbbc_require}
the delete accepted (HTTP `200`, returning a long-running operation).
.. NOTE: The delete cascades to every provider under the pool — no separate provider delete, the structural difference from
{rbtf_jilt}.

//axhos_step
. *Verify teardown*:
.. {rbbc_call}
link:https://cloud.google.com/iam/docs/reference/rest/v1/locations.workforcePools/get[workforcePools.get] for the pool resource name.
.. {rbbc_poll}
the pool at `RBGC_EVENTUAL_CONSISTENCY_SEC` intervals up to `RBGC_MAX_CONSISTENCY_SEC`.
.. {rbbc_require}
a razed terminal: `state: DELETED` *or* a `404` (gone). Either is success — the verify
tolerates whether the GET surfaces a soft-deleted pool, mirroring the depot-unmake
resource-state poll
({rbtgo_depot_unmake}).

//axhoot_typed_output
{rbtgi_manor}
. *Confirm Raze*:
.. {rbbc_show}
single-line banner naming the
{rbtgi_manor},
the razed pool resource name, the razed terminal reached, and the re-found recovery hint
(the manor-setup finisher founds a fresh pool under the standing id).

//axhoc_completion
Successful execution leaves the manor's workforce pool razed: the pool and every provider
that hung beneath it are gone, and no
{rbtf_citizen}
under the manor can
{rbtf_avow}
until the manor-setup finisher
({rbtgo_manor_found})
founds the pool afresh and
{rbtf_affiance}
re-seats the providers. The org-level grant, the
{rbtf_terrier}
bucket, and the depot projects stand untouched: raze reaches the pool alone.
