== Core Structure

A
{mcm_concept_model}
creates a cohesive gestalt - a unified constellation of ideas whose relationships and interactions transcend their individual definitions.
Through carefully curated terminology and precise linking patterns, each
{mcm_concept_model}
illuminates the deep conceptual relationships that give meaning to a domain.

The power of a
{mcm_concept_model}
lies in its ability to:

* Reveal the natural connections between ideas
* Preserve the integrity of concept relationships through evolution
* Enable clear visualization of how changes to one concept ripple through the constellation
* Guide understanding through carefully mapped idea spaces

Each
{mcm_concept_model}
manifests through a
{mcm_document}
that follows strict organizational patterns.
These patterns ensure that as the constellation evolves, their relationships remain clear and traceable.

The
{mcm_document}
contains two primary constrained element types:

* A
{mcm_mapping_section}
that defines all
{mcm_linked_term_s}
* One or more
{mcm_definition_section_s}
that provide precise meaning for each term

Through these elements, the
{mcm_document}
maintains the delicate balance between rigorous structure and organic concept evolution, allowing the idea constellation to grow while preserving its essential relationships.

== Document Composition

A
{mcm_concept_model}
of any size is rarely one file.
It composes into a fileset on four levels, each a unit of the manuscript that holds it.
The levels name what the include tree already builds; naming them lets a voicing bind a part to its whole and declare what binds.

[[mcm_sheaf]]
{mcm_sheaf}::
A subordinate detail treatment included into a
{mcm_codex} —
one gathering of the larger work.
A
{mcm_sheaf}
consumes its
{mcm_codex_p}
vocabulary rather than minting its own, carrying no
{mcm_mapping_section}
of its own, and binds to its
{mcm_codex}
by naming it back.
Every
{mcm_sheaf}
declares exactly one normativeness status — the axial status dimension — both at its head and at its inclusion point.

[[mcm_codex]]
{mcm_codex}::
The root file of a
{mcm_concept_model}:
the head that owns the
{mcm_mapping_section},
defines the vocabulary, and lists its parts — but holds none of the detail.
The connective mesentery of the document family: the structure that gives the parts their shape and shared lexicon without being the organs themselves.
A
{mcm_codex}
includes its
{mcm_sheaf_s}
and is the authoritative source from which they draw their terms.
First instinct reads "codex" as the whole bound book; here it names the binding head alone — the whole assembled work is the
{mcm_pandect}.

[[mcm_pandect]]
{mcm_pandect}::
One whole assembled
{mcm_concept_model}:
a
{mcm_codex}
together with all its
{mcm_sheaf_s},
the complete treatise on its subject.
The
{mcm_pandect}
is the rarely-named level — work happens in the
{mcm_codex}
and its
{mcm_sheaf_s};
the assembled whole is referenced seldom.

[[mcm_canon]]
{mcm_canon}::
The entire body of every
{mcm_pandect} —
the whole library of a project's specifications, taken as one authoritative collection.
As the literary canon is the collection of all admitted works, the
{mcm_canon}
is the collection of all
{mcm_pandect_s}.

== Text Organization

Text within a
{mcm_document}
follows specific structural rules to enable clear tracking of changes and relationships.

=== Line Break Fundamentals

The most fundamental organizing principle is the
{mcm_line_break}
.
Each
{mcm_line_break}
creates a clear boundary in the
{mcm_document}
while preserving fluid rendering in the output document.

==== Basic Line Break Rules

{mcm_line_break_s}
occur in these precise patterns:

* Between independent sentences
* Before and after each
{mcm_linked_term}
(including each term within a sequence)
* Between fragments that surround
{mcm_linked_term_s}

Basic example showing sentence and term breaks:

[source,asciidoc]
----
The system initializes its components.
The
{excm_processor}
begins operation.
Each component reports its status.
----

==== Term Integration Patterns

When incorporating
{mcm_linked_term_s}
the text follows these specific patterns:

Single term integration - breaks before and after the term:

[source,asciidoc]
----
The system processes
{excm_request}
according to defined rules.
----

Multiple
{mcm_linked_term_s}
in sequence - each term gets its own line, with commas attached:

[source,asciidoc]
----
The interface supports
{excm_drag},
{excm_drop},
and
{excm_scroll}
operations.
----

Complex
{mcm_linked_term}
relationships - each standalone term gets breaks:

[source,asciidoc]
----
The
{excm_validator}
applies each
{excm_rule}
to ensure
{excm_compliance}
with system requirements.
----

These patterns apply consistently across all document contexts, including:

* Regular paragraphs
* List items
* Definition entries
* Section introductions

=== Paragraph Organization

Paragraphs in the
{mcm_document}
follow these structural principles:

* {mcm_blank_line_s}
appear only between true paragraphs
* No
{mcm_blank_line_s}
between sentences within a paragraph
* No
{mcm_blank_line_s}
around source code blocks or list items
* Each paragraph focuses on a single concept or relationship

Example paragraph structure:

[source,asciidoc]
----
The system initializes its core components.
Each component performs self-diagnostics.
The
{excm_validator}
confirms system health.

The initialization process then moves to external systems.
The
{excm_connector}
establishes required links.
Each connection undergoes verification.
----

=== Special Cases

==== List Items

The
{mcm_document}
presents list items
with proper term integration
and conceptual breaks:

[source,asciidoc]
----
* The first item spans
{excm_component}
boundaries
* A second item demonstrates
{excm_process}
flow
* The final item shows
{excm_result}
formatting
----

==== Definition Entries

Definition entries follow standard sentence breaks while maintaining definition list structure:

The single space following `::` is necessary.

[source,asciidoc]
----
[[excm_term]]
{excm_example}:: 
The primary definition establishes core meaning.
Additional sentences expand understanding.
The
{excm_linked_term}
shows relationship handling.
----

==== Source Code Boundaries

Source code blocks establish strict boundaries for
{mcm_curation_action_s}.
These boundaries protect code content while maintaining proper term integration in surrounding text:

[source,asciidoc]
----
The
{excm_processor}
implements this interface:

[source,java]
-----
public interface Processor {
    void process();
    Status check();
}
-----

The implementation ensures
{excm_compliance}
with system requirements.
----

{mcm_curation_action_s}
respect these boundaries by:

* Maintaining term integration and line breaks in surrounding text
* Treating code block contents as opaque
* Preserving code formatting without modification
* Excluding code content from term matching and promotion analysis

This boundary pattern ensures code examples remain stable through document evolution while maintaining proper conceptual integration in their context.

=== Punctuation Rules

Punctuation follows standard English conventions regardless of line breaks:

* Sentence-ending punctuation stays with the sentence
* Commas remain with their preceding phrase
* Quotation marks enclose their content without breaks
* Parentheses maintain content unity

Example punctuation handling:

[source,asciidoc]
----
The system (version 2.0) activates its components.
The
{excm_processor}
begins its "primary sequence."
After initialization,
the system reports "ready."
----

=== Document Mechanics

=== {mcm_mapping_section} Constraints

The
{mcm_mapping_section}
enforces alignment rules to enhance readability and change tracking:

* The `<<` of each
{mcm_domain}
aligns to columns that are multiples of 10
* When entries require wider alignment, all lines adjust to the next larger multiple
* {mcm_attribute_reference_s}
maintain consistent indentation

[source,asciidoc]
----
// tag::mapping-example[]
:excm_doc_short:              <<excm_short,Short Term>>
:excm_doc_longer_term:        <<excm_longer,Longer Term>>
:excm_doc_much_longer_term:   <<excm_much_longer,Much Longer Term>>
// end::mapping-example[]
----

{mcm_normalization}
maintains these alignments during curation unless expansion is required.

=== Document Source Management

The
{mcm_document}
maintains compatibility with line-based tools
while preserving semantic structure.

This means:

* Favoring AsciiDoc's native list markers
over hardcoded sequence numbers

* Preserving processor directives
that enable clean rendering

* Ensuring changes remain visible
in both source differencing tools
and rendered comparisons

The goal is maintaining a document that:

* Shows ancestry clearly in source
* Renders cleanly through AsciiDoc
* Displays changes meaningfully in diffs
* Supports post-rendering analysis

=== Term Hierarchy Management

When
{mcm_linked_term_s}
appear to form hierarchical relationships, the
{mcm_document}
flattens this hierarchy through specific patterns:

* Each
{mcm_linked_term_s}
receives first-class presentation in the
{mcm_mapping_section}
* Related
{mcm_linked_term_s}
maintain separate definitions
* Relationships express through careful definition crafting

Example of hierarchical
{mcm_linked_term_s}
handling:

[source,asciidoc]
----
:excm_doc_validation:             <<excm_validation,Validation>>
:excm_doc_validation_rule:        <<excm_validation_rule,Validation Rule>>
:excm_doc_validation_set:         <<excm_validation_set,Validation Set>>
----

The definitions then express relationships:

[source,asciidoc]
----
[[excm_validation]]
{excm_doc_validation}:: 
The process of applying rules to ensure content correctness.

[[excm_validation_rule]]
{excm_doc_validation_rule}:: 
A specific constraint within the {excm_doc_validation} framework.

[[excm_validation_set]]
{excm_doc_validation_set}:: 
A collection of {excm_doc_validation_rule_s} that work together.
----

== Definition Patterns

=== Definition Form Patterns

Each
{mcm_linked_term}
in a
{mcm_document}
requires an anchor target and a definition.
The form determines how these elements are structured.

[[mcm_annotation]]
{mcm_annotation}::
A comment line providing type categorization or metadata about terms in a
{mcm_document}.
{mcm_annotation_s}
are ignored during first-order document reading.
When a referencing system is in context,
{mcm_annotation_s}
provide structured metadata interpreted by that system.
The content contains space-separated
{mcm_linked_term_s}
from the referencing system, which is typically out-of-context during first-order reading.
The grammar, interpretation, and placement rules for
{mcm_annotation_s}
are defined by the referencing system, not MCM.
+
{mcm_annotation_s}
use prefix-discriminated form: `//ax...` — no space after `//`, where the initial characters match a registered prefix identifying the referencing system.
The full content after `//` is interpreted by the referencing system as space-separated prefix terms.
+
The mechanical discriminator is the character immediately following `//`.
A space character indicates a regular comment.
A letter with no intervening space indicates an
{mcm_annotation}.
+
Registered prefixes:
+
* `ax` — Axial Lexicon

[[mcm_form_deflist]]
{mcm_form_deflist}::
The standard pattern using AsciiDoc definition list syntax.
The anchor target appears on its own line, followed by an optional
{mcm_annotation}
then the term reference with `::` and definition text.
Use for standalone terms with self-contained meaning.

[source,asciidoc]
----
[[term_anchor]]
//axk_motif
{term_reference}::
Definition text following sentence-per-line rules.
----

[[mcm_form_bullet]]
{mcm_form_bullet}::
A pattern for terms that belong to a parent structure or sequence.
Each term appears as a bullet item under its parent section.
The anchor target appears after the bullet marker, followed by an optional
{mcm_annotation}
then the term reference with definition prose.
Use for fields of a structure or steps in a procedure.

[source,asciidoc]
----
* [[field_anchor]]
//axr_member
{field_reference} defines the specific meaning within the parent structure.
----

[[mcm_form_section]]
{mcm_form_section}::
A pattern where the section heading itself is the term.
The anchor target appears before the section marker, followed by an optional
{mcm_annotation}
then the section heading containing the term reference.
The section content constitutes the definition.
Use for terms whose meaning requires extended explanation or contains nested child terms.

[source,asciidoc]
----
[[sequence_anchor]]
//axo_sequence
=== {sequence_reference}

The sequence executes these steps:

* [[step_one_anchor]]
//axo_step
First step description.
----

== Task Lens Support

{mcm_concept_model_s}
support focused work contexts through
{mcm_task_lens_es}
that emphasize relevant concepts while suppressing distractions.
See the
{mcm_task_lens_guide}
for compilation specifications and usage patterns.

=== Task Lens Structure

Each
{mcm_task_lens}
follows this pattern:

[source,asciidoc]
----
=== [Task Lens Name]

**Purpose**: [One sentence describing the work this lens enables]

**Gestalt**: [Metaphorical description guiding term interpretation]

**Core Activation**:

* {linked_term} - [Aspect emphasis if needed]
* {linked_term} - [Aspect emphasis if needed]

**Expanded Activation**:

* {linked_term} - [Reduced detail focus]
* {linked_term} - [Reduced detail focus]

**Suppressed Activation**:

* List of {linked_term_s} to remain dormant
* Additional suppression patterns

**Critical Relationships**:

* {term_a} ↔ {term_b}: [What aspect matters]
* {term_c} → {term_d}: [Specific mechanism focus]
----

Multiple
{mcm_task_lens_es}
may share terms but apply different
{mcm_focal_depth_s}
and aspect emphasis based on their purpose.

=== Blank Line Exception for Task Lenses

Despite the general document rule against
{mcm_blank_line_s}
around list items,
{mcm_task_lens}
sections require specific blank lines for valid asciidoc parsing:

* One blank line after each `**Section**:` header before its list
* One blank line between major sections
* No blank lines between list items within a section

This exception exists because asciidoc requires blank lines before bulleted lists to parse correctly, and the structured nature of
{mcm_task_lens_es}
makes the blank line placement predictable and consistent.

== Actor Definitions

[[mcm_editor]]
{mcm_editor}::
The human originator of
{mcm_concept_model_s}
and constraints.
The
{mcm_editor}
guides document evolution, establishes patterns, and directs transformations while maintaining conceptual integrity.

[[mcm_digital_mind]]
{mcm_digital_mind}::
An agent, typically an LLM, transforming
{mcm_document_s}
under
{mcm_editor}
direction and document constraints.
The
{mcm_digital_mind}
performs well-defined
{mcm_curation_action_s}
while preserving document patterns and relationships.

== {mcm_curation_action_s}

[[mcm_curation_action]]
{mcm_curation_action}::
A process executed by a
{mcm_digital_mind}
to adjust a
{mcm_document}
as directed by the
{mcm_editor}.
Each
{mcm_curation_action}
has a specific focus and constraints, modifying only those aspects of the
{mcm_document}
that fall within its defined scope.

[[mcm_normalization]]
{mcm_normalization}::
A
{mcm_curation_action}
that brings a
{mcm_document}
to canonical form through two phases.

== Lapidary

Vocabulary is the primary interface of every system the
{mcm_editor}
choreographs:
names are how intent survives across minds and sessions,
and the specification machinery around them is scaffolding in their service.
A name that fails its reader fails the system,
however sound the code beneath it.

Prefix discipline governs the left half of every persistent name;
this section governs the right half — the word itself.
The rules below bind the word part of every
{mcm_quoin},
the display text of every
{mcm_linked_term},
and any persistent name a system mints: verbs, nouns, file words, command words.
They constrain births, not the living —
existing names are swept only by deliberate remint tooling, never piecemeal.

* *Mint follows API.*
A concept earns a
{mcm_quoin}
only when a consumer must refer to it across a boundary —
never merely because the author finds it distinct.
Slots before words:
a slot is named and carried with candidates, alternates, and ledgered disqualifications
so dead ends are not re-tried,
and elections run their gates at deliberate moments, never mid-mulling.
* *Semantic uniqueness.*
One meaning per word across all persistent names, repo-wide.
A word already bound to a meaning anywhere in the system is unavailable for a second meaning, however distant the domain.
* *One word per concept.*
The dual of semantic uniqueness: no alias
{mcm_quoin_s} —
a concept already named is closed to a second word.
Near neighbors must be spaced:
adjacent definitions state their distinction, or one of the pair dies.
* *Vocabulary isolation.*
Words mint from source domains alien to software, cloud infrastructure, CI/CD, and corporate project management.
A word with heavy ambient presence in software prose is a
{mcm_trodden_word}
and is disqualified regardless of semantic fit.
Domain choice makes collision structurally impossible;
word-level checks merely verify.
* *The grep gate.*
A candidate word is verified by repo-wide grep before adoption;
a clean landing is a minting precondition, not a nicety.
* *Rare but real.*
Prefer rare, real words whose true meaning does semantic work.
Concrete, physical, sensory images lodge across sessions in ways abstract labels cannot.
Abbreviations spend recognition to save characters — the wrong trade.
* *Word husbandry.*
A word's commonness matches its operation's traffic:
daily surfaces earn short, plain, instantly-guessable words;
rare ceremonies must not spend them —
mint longer, rarer, more colorful words there,
conserving the small stock of plain English for the surfaces that need it.
Husbandry lowers the commonness ceiling, never the
{mcm_fair_faced}
floor: color may rise with rarity; opacity may not.
* *Sibling initials.*
Wherever a sibling set shares an abbreviation namespace —
colophon suffixes, quoin sub-letters, any single-character discriminator —
elect words whose first letters stay distinct,
so the abbreviation can remain the word's initial.
The verbs orbiting one critical noun are siblings from birth,
abbreviation namespace or not:
verb families acquire one eventually, and by then the words are minted.
An election-time check beside the grep gate;
the word-side twin of sub-letter monosemy.
* *Mint into an* {mcm_asterism}.
A new word joins a coherent metaphor family wherever one fits;
a lone orphan word is weaker than a family member.
* *Audible register.*
Sibling subsystems ride
{mcm_asterism_s}
whose registers are distinguishable by ear,
so a stray term's home system is audible without lookup.

[[mcm_asterism]]
{mcm_asterism}::
A named metaphor family whose source-domain relationships mirror the system relationships it names.
The name is the gestalt: a recognizable figure of stars that is not itself a constellation and may span several —
as a vocabulary family may span
{mcm_concept_model_s}.
Each term inherits relational meaning from the family
(the coronet sits below the firemark as the hoof marking sits below the brand),
so the metaphor performs structural work beyond retrieval,
and a new term in an established family arrives nearly pre-learned —
for the
{mcm_digital_mind}
and the
{mcm_editor}
alike.
Founding specimen: Job Jockey's equestrian vocabulary (JJS0, Equestrian Vocabulary), with the racing and dressage layers as sibling families sharing one register.
Model-side rationale: `Memos/retired/memo-20260610-quoin-minting-introspection.md`;
prefix-side sibling: `Memos/memo-20260110-acronym-selection-study.md`.

=== The Soil Family

Five states of ground name the ways a word can be unavailable for minting.
Two *disqualify* a word for a defect: a
{mcm_trodden_word}
fires the wrong valence — over-walked in a specific domain — and a
{mcm_leached_word}
fires none at all, worn smooth by universal use.
Three *reserve* a sound word by deliberate choice, withholding it from minting — two so a resource stays available, one until the concept that will bear it arrives;
a reserved word is not unmintable in itself, and the census of each grows by discovery rather than being settled in advance.
The disqualified pair are also
{mcm_antipattern_s},
cross-listed there.

[[mcm_trodden_word]]
{mcm_trodden_word}::
An
{mcm_antipattern}:
a persistent name minted from vocabulary worn featureless by ambient software prose —
open, close, run, lock, task, manager, handler, grant, stamp, pin.
The name is the gestalt: a path so heavily walked that no single footprint is findable on it;
grep returns a thousand strangers.
A
{mcm_trodden_word}
fails retrieval for the
{mcm_digital_mind}
and search for the
{mcm_editor},
and invites the plan-versus-product bleed that vocabulary isolation exists to prevent.
Semantic fit never rescues it:
the word is disqualified before fit is weighed.

[[mcm_leached_word]]
{mcm_leached_word}::
An
{mcm_antipattern}:
a persistent name minted from vocabulary worn so smooth by *universal* use that it fires no valence at all —
content, data, item, thing, value, object, element.
The name is the gestalt: ground leached of every nutrient, giving nothing back.
Where a
{mcm_trodden_word}
evokes the *wrong* thing — a specific ambient domain that floods the grep —
a
{mcm_leached_word}
evokes *nothing*: too generic to lodge in a reader or to do semantic work.

[[mcm_hallowed_word]]
{mcm_hallowed_word}::
A word withheld from minting because it carries strong resonance to the
{mcm_digital_mind_p}
nature, such that enclosing it in one frozen meaning would dull a resource the collaboration relies on.
The name is the gestalt: hallowed ground, set apart — freely walked, never parceled.
Reserved to protect the *word's* resonance.
The tell that a word belongs here is a redefinition in context that lands with a jarring loss of valence;
the
{mcm_digital_mind}
is a poor judge of its own reserves, since its nature is to adopt the
{mcm_editor_p}
word-use quickly, so the census grows by noticing surprise, not by introspection.
Seed: spine, axis, seam, sweep, proof, probe.
Standing conflict: probe is seeded here while still minted in
{mcm_cold_probe}
— the reservation is claimed ahead of the eviction, deferred until the global-rename tooling matures enough to sweep the live use.
Seed also, by term-of-art reservation — each already spoken in exactly its industry sense
and reserved to it, never re-minted for another meaning:
oracle, the mechanism that decides whether observed behavior is correct
(the chapbook Oracle lines, the theurge suite-composition oracle);
provenance, the recorded origin that grounds trust in an artifact
(the plumb and vouch surface, the provenance chapbook, a bridle designation persisting
through close);
census, a living roster produced by fresh survey rather than kept as a registry
(grep-as-census in the minting rules, the behavioral tweak census, the farrier op census,
the Matricula inscription census — and this very family's census, which grows by discovery);
membrane, the single contained place where interior rules bend to absorb a surveyed
foreign signature
(the Palisade conduct in the rules of engagement, the rivet kinds' Palisade membrane).

[[mcm_fallow_word]]
{mcm_fallow_word}::
A word withheld from minting because the collaboration keeps it in active use for context-painting — improvised explanation that needs the word to stay fluid and polysemous.
The name is the gestalt: fallow ground, left as commons rather than enclosed, so it stays freely grazable.
The grazing is joint — the
{mcm_editor}
and the
{mcm_digital_mind}
alike.
Minting encloses a word into one meaning;
a
{mcm_fallow_word}
resists enclosure to remain a brush on the collaboration's palette.
Reserved to protect the collaboration's explanatory reach, where
{mcm_hallowed_word}
protects the word's resonance.
Seed: mesentery, constellation, moniker, corpus (a body of documents spoken of as one),
lean (the collaboration's noun for a provisional
preference — "the lean on record" — fluid by need, never to be enclosed as a minted state),
fork (its noun for an unresolved branch point — "open forks, the mulling surface" — twin to lean
in shape and in reservation),
bank (its verb for depositing a thought where it outlives the current context — "banked in the
sheaf" — already load-bearing in normative prose, reserved here before that prose hardens into a mint),
blast (its root for scope-of-damage and the containment of it — "blast radius", "blast shield" —
never spoken bare, always productive across compounds, so enclosing the bare word would strand both),
gloss (its noun and verb for the one-line explanation that stands a concept up in place — "reference
the home, gloss in one line", "its inline gloss" — already the explanatory brush in normative prose,
reserved before that prose hardens into a mint).

[[mcm_furrowed_word]]
{mcm_furrowed_word}::
A word withheld from minting now but earmarked for a future mint: liked and claimed, held until the concept that will bear it arrives.
The name is the gestalt: furrowed ground, the trench cut and waiting for the seed.
Distinct from its reserve-siblings by *intent to mint* — where
{mcm_hallowed_word}
and
{mcm_fallow_word}
are withheld to stay unminted (one to protect the word's resonance, one the collaboration's reach), a
{mcm_furrowed_word}
is enclosure merely deferred: it will be minted once its concept is ready, or released if the intent lapses.
Seed: tendon, blazon.

=== Exposure

Word-selection care scales with who must read the name.
Every persistent name sits on a ladder of increasing dressing:

* {mcm_hearting} — interior names, uncatalogued, governed by prefix discipline alone.
* A
{mcm_quoin}
— the catalogued default; the rules above bind.
* {mcm_ashlar} — a
{mcm_quoin}
that faces the operator; everything below binds, plus the rules at its definition.

The ladder is deliberate at both ends:
{mcm_hearting}
is permission to leave interior names plain,
granted so that care can concentrate where reading is costly.
Misclassification fails quiet in one direction —
a word that should be
{mcm_ashlar}
but is unmarked skips the stricter tests —
so when a word surfaces in operator output, ask whether it belongs on the
{mcm_broadside}.

The ladder sorts by exposure — who must read the name.
A second, finer mark cuts across it: *centrality*, how much of the user's model a name carries.
The few
{mcm_ashlar_s}
a user reasons *through* are
{mcm_bondstone_s};
the verbs that work them are
{mcm_toothing}.
Both draw a care beyond the ordinary
{mcm_ashlar}:
a model is held by its
{mcm_bondstone_s},
and a name that fails there fails the whole model, not a single reading.

[[mcm_ashlar]]
{mcm_ashlar}::
A
{mcm_quoin}
the operator must trigger, observe in output or errors, or reason about system state with.
The dressed facing stone — finished finely because it faces out.
Beyond the rules above, an
{mcm_ashlar}
must be
{mcm_fair_faced},
draws from the
{mcm_coffer},
and is not minted until it appears on the project's
{mcm_broadside}.
Scrutiny scales with contact frequency:
a word the operator meets daily outranks a founding-ceremony word,
and both outrank any spec-interior
{mcm_quoin}.
Verbs name what the operator does or sees, never the mechanism beneath.
Words appearing in failure output are
{mcm_ashlar_s}
even when the operator never invokes them.

[[mcm_bondstone]]
{mcm_bondstone}::
The few
{mcm_ashlar_s}
a user reasons *through* — the small set of operator-facing names that constitute the user's working model of the system, as against a name merely surfaced once in output.
The through-stone of the wall: dressed on the face like any
{mcm_ashlar},
yet reaching the wall's full thickness to bind the visible face to the unseen
{mcm_hearting} —
so a
{mcm_bondstone}
ties what the operator sees to what the system is.
Distinguished from a plain
{mcm_ashlar}
not by exposure — both face out — but by centrality: an
{mcm_ashlar}
in a rare error message faces the operator yet bears no model, where a
{mcm_bondstone}
is the name the user's whole model hangs on.
Every rule above binds hardest here.
It must above all be
{mcm_fair_faced}
and lodge unaided; it must dodge overloading in both general software prose and the system's own adjacent domain; and it earns first-letter separation from its siblings, so the set an operator holds at once stays distinct in the hand and the head.

[[mcm_toothing]]
{mcm_toothing}::
The verb-tier sibling of the
{mcm_bondstone}:
the operator-facing acts performed upon the model-constituting names, and the operations those names visibly perform upon one another so the system stays legible as it works.
The toothing-stones left projecting at a wall's end, the worked interlock by which a later course keys in — so a growing system's new names bond into the standing set rather than abutting it loose.
A
{mcm_toothing}
verb draws the same heightened care as the
{mcm_bondstone}
it acts on: it must be
{mcm_fair_faced}
and lodge unaided, dodge overloading in software prose and the adjacent domain, and hold first-letter separation across the set an operator wields at once.
A
{mcm_toothing}
is the act-concept, never the bare word:
its definition declares a subject slot and an object slot,
each explicitly valued — a
{mcm_bondstone}
(or a supertype
{mcm_quoin}
where several children legitimately serve),
the operator, or none —
so a deliberate intransitive is never mistaken for a forgotten declaration.
The verb word is the
{mcm_toothing}
minted name — one
{mcm_quoin}
carrying act and word together, never a second
{mcm_quoin}
for the word alone.
A different subject or object — none included — is a different act and takes a different word:
a verb is never overloaded.
The definition opens with its canonical clause — subject, verb, object in one working sentence —
so first contact teaches the frame the word rides in.

[[mcm_hearting]]
{mcm_hearting}::
The wall-core fill — structural, unseen, taking no dressing.
Interior names governed by prefix discipline alone:
locals, private helpers, intermediate artifacts no operator and no spec must read.
{mcm_hearting}
is a deliberate grant of plainness, not neglect;
its existence is what lets
{mcm_ashlar}
care stay affordable.

[[mcm_fair_faced]]
{mcm_fair_faced}::
The property an
{mcm_ashlar}
must hold: finished so it needs no render coat.
An operator meeting the word for the first time, mid-ceremony,
must act correctly without a glossary lookup.
Rare enough to land clean in the grep gate,
common enough to guess warm —
the deliberate tightening of rare-but-real at the wall's face.
The
{mcm_cold_probe}
is its test.

[[mcm_coffer]]
{mcm_coffer}::
The bounded budget of
{mcm_ashlar_s}
an operator can be asked to hold.
Each mint spends from it;
spec-grain precision collapses into fewer operator words wherever the distinction does not change what the operator does.
The fine grain lives on in
{mcm_quoin_s};
the
{mcm_coffer}
governs only the wall's face.

[[mcm_broadside]]
{mcm_broadside}::
The single public sheet where a project posts its
{mcm_ashlar}
vocabulary.
Each project designates exactly one;
presence on the
{mcm_broadside}
is the registry contract —
an
{mcm_ashlar}
mint is incomplete until its entry exists.
A project whose operators work through digital minds may mirror the sheet into agent context files;
mirrors are derived, never canonical.

[[mcm_cold_probe]]
{mcm_cold_probe}::
A
{mcm_curation_action}
testing
{mcm_fair_faced}:
present each candidate to a fresh reader in one sentence of working context;
the reader states what they expect the word to do or mean.
The stated expectation is the verdict —
mismatch fails the candidate,
and no advocacy by the minter is admitted.
The verdict resolves into three outcomes, ranked:
a reader who acts correctly (the word is
{mcm_fair_faced});
a reader who draws a blank but knows to seek context (safe — the blank is self-correcting);
and a reader who acts *confidently on the wrong meaning* — a *false friend*, the worst outcome, because it is self-confirming and silently in error.
A false friend is worse than a blank.
The bar is exposure-dependent:
an
{mcm_ashlar}
must reach the first outcome, since no glossary is at hand when it surfaces in output;
a spec-interior
{mcm_quoin}
need only avoid the third, since its definition is at hand to turn a blank into knowledge.
A word that draws a blank but evokes the right image is therefore a safe spec-interior mint, where a confidently-misread one is not.

=== The Clause

An operation faces its reader as a clause — subject, verb, object —
and the grammar above the words is itself governed.
The
{mcm_toothing}
declaration is canonical;
every rendering is a projection derived from it,
adding nothing the declaration lacks,
so the dressed surfaces cannot drift from the word or from one another —
the word is stored truth, the clause its dress.
A subject is rendered only where it distinguishes:
an act any hand may perform shows verb and object alone,
while an act performed under an assumed authority names that authority,
because there the subject is the permission model made visible.
The face law below consumes this rule: a
{mcm_revetment}
showing an operation shows its projection, never its declaration.

Not every operation name seats a
{mcm_toothing}.
Where a common verb is a transparent functor over its object —
list, render, validate; the object carrying all the meaning —
the compound operation name is the minted unit,
the verb slot stays plain English,
and no verb
{mcm_quoin}
exists.
A
{mcm_toothing}
is earned exactly where the verb must carry what its object cannot —
blast radius, custody, trust, posture: the discriminating load —
and the
{mcm_trodden_word}
ban resolves at the unit of minting:
disqualified as a name, a common verb may still serve inside a compound whose whole is the name,
or stand in the catalog as a categorical coordinate on the minted verb's record —
never as the rendered verb of a minted act,
lest six distinct destructions flatten back into one "delete."

=== The Revetment

The rules above govern names as the system mints them;
one surface governs names as the system *ships* them.
A veiled interior — specs held back, their lapidary rigor read by no outside eye —
still needs a public face,
and that face is prose an outsider reads with none of the interior at hand.
The
{mcm_revetment}
is that maintained face,
and the law below is what it answers to:
what the interior may say when it dresses itself for the outside.

The face has a reader who holds no glossary and never will,
so its law runs stricter than the ladder's —
only names already dressed for that reader may cross, and nothing of the dressing itself.

* *Ashlar-or-prose only.*
Every token on the face is an
{mcm_ashlar}
in display form or plain prose;
an interior name — a bare
{mcm_quoin}
or a
{mcm_hearting} —
reaching the face is a
{mcm_spall}.
* *Broadside-complete.*
Every
{mcm_ashlar}
the face uses resolves to its entry on the
{mcm_broadside};
a face word with no registry home is a face that cannot be read.
* *References resolve.*
Every cross-reference on the face lands on a declared anchor;
a link into nothing spalls the structure rather than the word.
* *Register silence.*
The lapidary meta-register — the vocabulary of this craft itself — never crosses to the face.
The reader is shown the wall, never the mason's word for dressing it.

The last invariant turns on itself:
{mcm_revetment}
and
{mcm_spall}
are lapidary meta-register too,
so the words that name the face and its defect never appear on a
{mcm_revetment}.

[[mcm_revetment]]
{mcm_revetment}::
The maintained public face of a veiled interior:
a shipped prose surface presenting a closed
{mcm_concept_model} —
or a whole closed system —
in dressed, reader-legible form.
The facing course laid across the raw earthwork,
finishing what shows while shielding what stands behind,
so its work is armor and not ornament.
It answers the face law above;
its type specimen is a project's public README.

[[mcm_spall]]
{mcm_spall}::
The face defect:
an interior name, or the lapidary meta-register itself,
surfacing on the
{mcm_revetment}
and breaking its dressed face to expose the core.
Masonry spalling — a fragment split from the facing,
the raw fill showing through the dressed stone.
Each invariant of the face law forbids one way a face can spall.

== Load-Bearing Complexity

[[mcm_load_bearing]]
{mcm_load_bearing}::
The property an element has when its removal would open a gap between intent and behavior.
Every element the collaboration keeps — a definition line, a function extraction, a pattern variant, a structural distinction, a tolerated input form — must carry weight,
and the litmus for any new one is whether it earns its existence at all.
Where similar things differ, the difference is either
{mcm_load_bearing},
and then documented as such, or it is not, and then homogenized.
A non-{mcm_load_bearing} element buys no correctness and charges cognitive rent forever;
each is small, and their aggregate is what makes a system incomprehensible.
This is the headwater principle the
{mcm_antipattern}
catalog instantiates:
every entry in that catalog names one recurring way an element fails to carry the weight it claims —
a restatement that is not the law, a decode that is not a boundary, a fork that is not a decision.
Domain instances state it in their own terms:
BCG's Zeroes Theory and Interface Contamination Discipline for input and version space,
ACG's Allodial Discipline for values and names,
and RCG for Rust's constructor and constant surfaces.
The instances differ in what they measure; the prior question they all answer is this one.

== Antipatterns

[[mcm_antipattern]]
{mcm_antipattern}::
A named, recurring defect formally catalogued so that
{mcm_curation_action_s}
and reviews can cite it precisely.
The catalog spans document defects — flaws in durable artifacts —
delivery defects — failures in operator-facing presentation —
and construction defects — flaws in the built form of a system.
All recur, and all are dischargeable only once named.
Naming the defect is what makes it detectable and dischargeable;
an unnamed defect recurs because no rule can forbid it.
An entry here owes the name, the general form, the recognition tells, and why the defect recurs —
diagnosis only, and language-free.
Treatment is not owed here and does not belong here:
it is owed by each
{mcm_refraction}.

[[mcm_refraction]]
{mcm_refraction}::
The appearance of one catalogued
{mcm_antipattern}
inside a single guide, bent into that guide's dialect:
the shape the defect takes in that language, and the canonical repair for it there.
MCM diagnoses; the guide treats.
A
{mcm_refraction}
is self-sufficient by construction — a coding agent working inside a guide is never sent out of it to learn the cure,
and carries the quoin name in backticks as its citation, which is the whole of what it owes back to the catalog.
That backticked name is the cross-format handle between an AsciiDoc catalog and Markdown guides that cannot resolve its anchors,
and grep over it is the registry of where the defect has been refracted:
the catalog entry keeps no list of the guides that cite it, since such a list is a second home that drifts.
A
{mcm_refraction}
is born when a guide first has the disease to treat, never speculatively —
an entry with no live instance in a dialect earns nothing there.

[[mcm_molehill]]
{mcm_molehill}::
An
{mcm_antipattern}:
a delivery defect — manufacturing a significant-looking decision-fork or challenge
out of something nearly inconsequential,
then building structure around it.
The name is the gestalt: a molehill presented at mountain scale,
spending the reader's deliberation on stakes that fit in a clause.

[[mcm_phantom_wire]]
{mcm_phantom_wire}::
An
{mcm_antipattern}:
a construction defect — data born and consumed inside one program is flattened into an encoded string and re-parsed to be read,
when the language offered a structural form that would have held it.
The name is the gestalt: the encoding is a wire format, and there is no wire —
serialization is the price paid at a boundary, and here no boundary was crossed.
The recognition tells are an *encode/decode pair with nothing between them*:
a literal whose fields are joined by a delimiter, a parse expression that splits them apart,
and no process, file, socket, or foreign reader standing in the gap.
The failures all live in the decode, and all are quiet.
A record missing its delimiter decodes *successfully* into garbage — commonly the whole record taken as every field —
so a malformation becomes a wrong answer rather than an error.
The delimiter's illegality in the payload is a constraint no code states and nothing enforces.
The field shape exists only inside the parse expressions, so it is re-derived at each one and can disagree between them.
The defect recurs because the delimited-string table is one of the most heavily-worn shapes in the training corpus,
so it arrives as a
{mcm_digital_mind_p}
first reflex, feeling like the obvious way to write a table down;
naming it is what licenses the
{mcm_digital_mind}
to distrust that reflex.
The cure is always the same in shape — hold the record in the structure the language has, and validate at construction so a bad record dies loud where it is written — and always dialect-specific in form,
so it is stated by each
{mcm_refraction}
and not here.
Bash refraction: BCG, Representation axis.

[[mcm_skidmark]]
{mcm_skidmark}::
An
{mcm_antipattern}:
version commentary embedded in a durable document — revision markers, dated change narration, "new in vX" annotations.
The name is the gestalt: the streak left by something that already passed, saying nothing about where it is now.
History-time knowledge belongs to the commit;
git log and blame carry every line's date and reason with a forcing function, while in-document
{mcm_skidmark_s}
drift and pollute present-tense reading.
A durable document — spec, guide, code, context file — speaks of itself only in the present tense.
A dated artifact — memo, scar, commit message — is itself a historical record and may carry revision metadata without fault.
Detection and drain moves are catalogued in ACG (ACGm_107).

[[mcm_stun]]
{mcm_stun}::
An
{mcm_antipattern}:
a delivery defect — a wall of dense, fully-structured prose with no on-ramp,
so the reader's comprehension seizes before it can enter.
The name is the gestalt: the delivery stuns before it informs.
The finished staircase, seen from outside, is a wall;
the defect is not completeness but delivering it whole,
with no sentence to stand on and no way to climb.

The word-minting defects —
{mcm_trodden_word}
and
{mcm_leached_word} —
are catalogued with their reserved kin in Lapidary's soil family, since their gestalt is a state of vocabulary rather than a delivery or document flaw.

[[mcm_word_cancer]]
{mcm_word_cancer}::
An
{mcm_antipattern}:
variations of one normative idea scattered across documents as paraphrases,
each restatement enlarging the drift surface,
none authoritative.
The name is deliberately ugly, and the ugliness is the diagnosis:
each paraphrase metastasizes —
a
{mcm_digital_mind}
completing "what should be said here" recreates a nearby idea in fresh words,
and every recreation seeds the next.
The cost is mechanical, not aesthetic:
near-paraphrases are nearly the same retrieval key,
and once they drift the
{mcm_digital_mind}
retrieves a confident blend of them — statement-level interference —
while the
{mcm_editor}
is left unable to know which wording is law.
Detection and collapse moves are catalogued in ACG (ACGm_105).

== Term Management Elements

[[mcm_attribute_reference]]
{mcm_attribute_reference}::
A variable-style reference that connects a
{mcm_category}
prefixed attribute name to a specific
{mcm_domain}
and replacement text potentially with multiple
{mcm_variant_s}.
The
{mcm_attribute_reference}
forms the mechanical foundation for
{mcm_linked_term}
implementation in the
{mcm_document}.
Each
{mcm_attribute_reference}
follows strict formatting rules defined in the
{mcm_mapping_section}.

[[mcm_quoin]]
{mcm_quoin}::
A formal identifier with full cataloguing in the
{mcm_concept_model}:
{mcm_attribute_reference}
in the
{mcm_mapping_section},
anchor at the definition site, and definition text.
The cornerstone that locks the structure — addressable from anywhere, tracked across the constellation.
A
{mcm_quoin}
is the Diptych-era term for what
{mcm_linked_term}
describes in MCM's AsciiDoc expression.

[[mcm_inlay]]
{mcm_inlay}::
A formal identifier following the prefix naming discipline that exists only within a subdocument's scope.
No
{mcm_attribute_reference},
no anchor, no
{mcm_mapping_section}
entry.
An
{mcm_inlay}
is set permanently into a surface — recognized by its prefix pattern — but not catalogued in the parent document's lexicon.
Used by hierarchy markers and annotation dimensions that need to formally identify elements without elevating them to
{mcm_quoin}
status.

[[mcm_sprue]]
{mcm_sprue}::
A literal wire-level token in backtick delimiters representing a serialized name.
No
{mcm_attribute_reference},
no anchor, no
{mcm_mapping_section}
entry, no prefix naming discipline required.
A
{mcm_sprue}
is the exact character sequence as it appears in a wire format — a JSON property key, an API field name, a protocol identifier.
May originate from external schemas or foreign conventions.
A
{mcm_sprue}
that follows the prefix naming discipline is also an
{mcm_inlay};
one that does not is recognized solely by its backtick delimiters.
Used by arity rules when annotations read literal wire names from following text.

[[mcm_rivet]]
{mcm_rivet}::
A formal identifier for a *normative proposition* — an invariant, a deliberate
deviation, a foreign-behavior signature, or a Palisade membrane — that a
{mcm_concept_model}
defines once at a definition site and that source code and tests cite by its
identifier.
Unlike a
{mcm_quoin},
whose readable name carries its meaning, a
{mcm_rivet}
identifier is *opaque*: it leaks no semantics into released code, while one
repo-wide search on the identifier still returns the definition, every
defending code site, and every attacking test — a census with no registry to
drift.
The kind of proposition is declared at the definition site by an
{mcm_annotation}
voicing an axial motif (the `axvc_` family), never encoded in the identifier,
so re-categorizing a
{mcm_rivet}
never re-mints it.
A
{mcm_rivet}
is a *voiced subject*, as is the
{mcm_quoin};
the
{mcm_inlay}
and
{mcm_sprue}
are by contrast *lookahead arguments* that voicings read, never voiced
subjects.
+
A
{mcm_rivet}
has exactly one definition site, placed by citation reach:
it lives in the
{mcm_sheaf}
that owns its subject while only that sheaf — plus source code and tests — cites it,
and hoists to the
{mcm_codex}
the moment a second
{mcm_sheaf}
cites it, because peers consume shared vocabulary from their
{mcm_codex},
never from a sibling's interior.
The hoist moves the definition prose alone:
the identifier is never re-minted, so every citing site — code, test, and spec alike — survives placement changes unedited.
A sibling document citing a
{mcm_rivet}
may orient its reader with at most one clause beside the identifier;
the mechanism and rationale live only at the definition site.
Until a linter enforces placement, any edit adding a cross-sheaf citation performs the hoist in the same move.

[[mcm_precept]]
{mcm_precept}::
A formal identifier for a numbered conformance rule that a coding guide defines
once and that tests cite to determine conformance.
The open cousin of the
{mcm_rivet}:
the same define-once, cite-by-search census with no registry to drift, but the
identifier is *readable and numbered* (CBi_101, JDo_101, ACGm_101) rather than
opaque — because a guide governs open conventions with nothing to hide, where a
{mcm_rivet}
guards closed reasoning that must not leak into released code.
A
{mcm_precept}
is *audit-anchored*: its conformance is sincerely determined by the tests that
cite it, and a deviation cites the
{mcm_precept}
it bends.
The two share the citation axis and differ only by exposure.
Kind-classification, should
{mcm_precept_s}
ever need it, is deferred to an axial voicing as the
{mcm_rivet}
has the `axvc_` family.

The cited-proposition signets classify by their terminal letter: `r` marks a
{mcm_rivet}
(`RBr_`), while each
{mcm_precept}
family takes a different letter (`CBi_`, `JDo_`, `ACGm_`).
The letter is centrally controlled, never discovered, so this namespace carries no terminal-exclusivity growth pressure; and the mixed case — uppercase acronym, lowercase terminal letter — sets the whole cited-proposition namespace apart from the lowercase code namespace and the all-caps constant namespace, a separation a scanner must read case-sensitively.

[[mcm_category]]
{mcm_category}::
A prefix mechanism that classifies
{mcm_linked_term_s}
by their conceptual role.
Each
{mcm_category}
must be declared in the
{mcm_mapping_section}
header and follows consistent patterns for classifying terms by type (e.g., 'doc_' for document elements, 'term_' for terminology elements).

[[mcm_variant]]
{mcm_variant}::
A systematic modification of a base term that maintains its core meaning while expressing a different form.
Common
{mcm_variant_s}
include plurals (_s), possessives (_p), and verb conjugations (_ed, _ing), each following patterns defined in the
{mcm_mapping_section}.

[[mcm_domain]]
{mcm_domain}::
A unique identifier that anchors a term's definition within the document structure.
Each
{mcm_domain}
uses snake case formatting and maintains consistent patterns (e.g., 'mcm_<canonical_term>') to enable reliable linking and reference.

[[mcm_task_lens]]
{mcm_task_lens}::
A focused activation pattern within a
{mcm_concept_model}

Each
{mcm_task_lens}
defines which
{mcm_linked_term_s}
activate at what
{mcm_focal_depth}
and guides interpretation through gestalt descriptions and critical relationship specifications.


[[mcm_task_lens_guide]]
{mcm_task_lens_guide}::
A separate guide document that specifies how to compile task-focused representations from a full

The
{mcm_task_lens_guide}

[[mcm_lens_specification]]
{mcm_lens_specification}::
The structured definition within a
{mcm_task_lens}
that categorizes
{mcm_linked_term_s}
by their
{mcm_focal_depth}

The
{mcm_lens_specification}
enables mechanical compilation of task-optimized representations from the full
{mcm_concept_model}.

[[mcm_focal_depth]]
{mcm_focal_depth}::
The activation level of a
{mcm_linked_term}
