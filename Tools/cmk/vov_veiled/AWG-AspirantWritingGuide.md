# Aspirant Writing Guide (AWG) — Refining an Aspirant Sheaf

v0 posture: **precepts inferred from a type specimen.** The founding session is the
tackle refinement of chat pym-beta-tackle (its notch stream, plus
`Memos/memo-20260707-tackle-polarity-and-graph.md` and
`Memos/memo-20260707-rbm-domain-census.md`, are the provenance corpus).
Refinement from further specimens is expected; the skeleton is GMG's.

## Purpose

AWG codifies how an agent–operator pair refines an aspirant sheaf — from
surgery-scarred nucleation toward settling — under MCM/AXLA law and the JJS0
mulling-heat doctrine. A guide, not a spec and not a handbook: it governs how
the *refinement session behaves*, not what any system does.

Siblings: MCM/AXLA (the spec-side law the session operates under), GMG (this
guide's own form), HCG (the neighbor genre — operator-facing procedure vs this
guide's author-facing discipline), ACG (reference-the-home, which several
precepts instantiate).

## Core Philosophy

- **Three homes, never blended.** The sheaf is law-in-formation and states only
  present law; the chat is the forge; git notches and memos are the record. A
  sheaf that journals is drifting toward the wrong home.
- **Exploration beats formalization.** A fork honestly open outranks a
  premature mint; representation commitments wait until the concept stops
  moving.
- **Simplicity over overfitting.** The concept wins by staying simple rather
  than fitted to today's problems.
- **The operator's reversal is signal.** Doctrine can lag reality — the session
  must surface the conflict sincerely, not defend the written law. (Type
  specimen: an identity store carried its type-marking glyphs on disk against
  written doctrine; the store was right, and the doctrine amendment followed.)

## Authored Disciplines

1. **Read the foundations whole, yourself.** MCM, AXLA, and the governing
   guides — in full, never from summaries or delegated digests. The sheaf's
   conventions (lintel, voicings, timeless law, mapping discipline) are
   load-bearing and are violated silently by an unread agent.

2. **Charter first.** Open the sheaf with the operator's one-sentence charter
   and hang every section off its parts. If the operator has not yet said it in
   one sentence, eliciting that sentence is the first work product.

3. **Work a real census.** Force the concept against the home repo's actual
   instances; the load-bearing discoveries come out of the worked example, not
   the abstraction. Concept and census fly in formation: the sheaf carries a
   frozen illustration; a living memo grows; the sheaf cites the memo (an
   unreferenced memo is undiscoverable).

4. **Mine failed specimens.** A dead prior attempt at the same theme is
   evidence. Autopsy it against the live tree — verify the rot, don't trust the
   corpse's own labels — and let the failure modes predict for the new design.

5. **One home or none.** No sibling half-homes inviting confabulation. A
   settled matter gets one section; everything else cites it — including across
   the sheaf/memo boundary.

6. **Mint follows API.** A concept earns a quoin when a consumer must refer to
   it across a boundary — never merely because the author finds it distinct.
   Slots before words; candidates carried with alternates and disqualifications
   ledgered so dead ends are never re-tried; elections run gates at deliberate
   moments. The grep gate includes the prefix registries: reservations live
   where code grep cannot see.

7. **Copy house precedent before inventing.** Serialization manners, identity
   law, sprue grammar — find the proven in-repo pattern, cite its file, copy
   its shape. The precedent search is also where doctrine-vs-practice conflicts
   surface (see Core Philosophy, reversal).

8. **Timeless prose, even here.** AXLA's aspirant exemption *permits*
   journaling; this practice declines it. The sheaf states present law; dates,
   attributions, and supersession narratives live in git notches and memos;
   skidmarks purge at settling milestones. (Whether this tightening becomes
   AXLA doctrine is an open question banked at its first practicing sheaf.)

9. **Bank nudges; never drive-by edits.** Obligations toward sibling sheaves
   and canon accrue in a Reconciliation section — with drafts where useful —
   and land later as deliberate acts. Canon amendments travel the CMK covenant:
   drafted in-sheaf, ratified in conversation, applied deliberately.

10. **Notch at rulings.** Commit at each settled decision with a rich intent
    line. The notch stream is the journal the timeless sheaf refuses to be.

11. **Build the settling register.** Before implementation: enumerate what must
    settle, each entry citing its substance's home, tagged with a drain mode by
    one test — *does the choice's outcome reshape the pace decomposition?*
    SHAPES THE CUT (settle before slating) / SETTLING PACE (early judgment
    pace) / RIDES A PACE (mechanical fold-in). Counterweight: never settle
    early what a mount agent decides better against live code (the docket
    anti-patterns govern that direction).

12. **Wind down deliberately.** Inventory the chat's unincorporated material;
    every item banks into the sheaf, becomes a register entry, or is declined
    with a stated reason. Nothing evaporates silently.

13. **Pace the operator.** One sentence to stand on before depth; dense
    deliverables trot (pre-counted chunks, context reminders, operator-paced
    advance); simplify language on the operator's signal. The delivery craft
    has its own home in the project's operator-context files — cite it, don't
    restate it.

14. **An aspirant sheaf is a sheaf — name it like one from birth.** It takes
    its codex's acronym scheme immediately (`JJSAB-breeze.adoc`,
    `JJSAT-tackle.adoc`), never a hyphenated words-basename (the JJ family's
    legacy basenames were the recorded anti-pattern, renamed away); a rename
    sweeps every live reference in the same act, leaving historical records
    untouched.

## Delivery (how this guide reaches a session)

AWG expects to ride the aspirant-sheaves tackle's *trappings*
(`Tools/jjk/vov_veiled/JJSAT-tackle.adoc`: the aspirant domain,
lintel-audited): a dispatched session whose target files resolve to that tackle
receives this guide in launch-time provisioning; an ad-hoc session reaches it
through the pull door on first touch. This is the same repair as the
read-BCG-before-bash case — the binding retires the operator's memory as the
enforcement mechanism.
